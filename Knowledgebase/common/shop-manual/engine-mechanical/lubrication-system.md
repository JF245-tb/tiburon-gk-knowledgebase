# Lubrication System

## Oil Pump

### Components <!-- EDHA9400 -->

<!-- Figure: Oil pump and oil pan exploded view showing oil pump cover, oil pump outer rotor, oil pump inner rotor, oil pump case, crank shaft sprocket, crank shaft oil seal, relief valve plunger, relief valve spring, relief valve plug, upper baffle plate, oil screen gasket, oil screen, lower baffle plate, upper oil pan, oil filter, oil pressure switch, and lower oil pan, source: EMA.pdf page 55 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Oil pump case bolt | 12-15 Nm (120-150 kg.cm, 9-11 lb.ft) |
| Oil pump cover screw | 8-12 Nm (80-120 kg.cm, 6-9 lb.ft) |
| Relief valve plug | 40-50 Nm (400-500 kg.cm, 29-36 lb.ft) |
| Oil screen bolt | 15-22 Nm (150-220 kg.cm, 11-16 lb.ft) |
| Oil pressure switch | 15-22 Nm (150-220 kg.cm, 11-16 lb.ft) |
| Lower oil pan bolts | 10-12 Nm (100-120 kg.cm, 7-9 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kg.cm, lb.ft)**

### Disassembly <!-- EDHA9500 -->

1. Remove the oil pressure switch, using 24 mm deep socket.

> **NOTE**
> *Since a sealant is used on the threaded area, be careful not to damage the oil pressure switch.*

2. Remove the oil filter and the oil pan.
3. Remove the oil screen and gasket.
4. Remove the three bracket securing bolts and remove the oil filter bracket and gasket.
5. Remove the oil relief valve plug from the oil pump case.
6. Remove the oil pump case.

<!-- Figure: Oil pressure switch removal with 24mm socket, sealant application detail, source: EMA.pdf page 56 -->

### Inspection <!-- ECUA7700 -->

#### Oil Pump

1. Visually check the parts of the oil pump case for cracks and damage.
2. Assemble the rotor on the oil pump and then check the clearance with a thickness gauge.

**Oil pump side clearance**

| Parameter | Standard Value |
|---|---|
| Body clearance | 0.100-0.181 mm (0.0039-0.0071 in.) |
| Side clearance | 0.040-0.095 mm (0.0016-0.0037 in.) |

<!-- Figure: Oil pump rotor clearance measurement with thickness gauge, showing body clearance and side clearance measurement points, source: EMA.pdf page 56 -->

#### Relief Plunger and Spring

1. Check the relief plunger for smooth operation.
2. Check the relief spring for deformation or breaks.

#### Oil Filter Bracket

1. Make sure there is no damage on the surface that mates with the oil filter.
2. Check the oil filter bracket for oil leaks or cracks.

#### Oil Pressure Switch

1. Check the continuity between the terminal and the body with an ohmmeter. If there is no continuity, replace the oil pressure switch.

<!-- Figure: Oil pressure switch terminal continuity test with ohmmeter, source: EMA.pdf page 56 -->

2. Check the continuity between the terminal and the body when the fine wire is pushed. If there is continuity even when the fine wire is pushed, replace the switch.

3. If there is no continuity when a 50 kPa (7 psi) vacuum is applied to the oil hole, the switch is operating properly. Check for air leakage. If air leaks, the diaphragm is broken. Replace it.

<!-- Figure: Oil pressure switch vacuum test with fine wire, source: EMA.pdf page 57 -->

**Operation Pressure**

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| Oil pressure switch | 20-40 kPa (0.2-0.4 kg/cm², 2.9-5.8 psi) |

### Reassembly <!-- ECHA8TC -->

1. Install the oil pump case with the gasket.

   **Tightening torque**

   | Fastener | Torque |
   |---|---|
   | Oil pump case bolt | 12-15 Nm (120-150 kg.cm, 9-11 lb.ft) |
   | Oil pump cover screw | 8-12 Nm (80-120 kg.cm, 6-9 lb.ft) |

<!-- Figure: Oil pump case front and rear views, source: EMA.pdf page 57 -->

2. Install the oil seal into the oil pump case as tightly as possible, using the special tool (09214-33000).

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09214-33000 installing oil seal into oil pump case, source: EMA.pdf page 57 -->

3. Install the relief plunger and spring, and tighten the oil relief valve plug to the specified torque.

   **Tightening torque**

   Oil relief valve plug:

   40-50 Nm (400-500 kg.cm, 29-36 lb.ft)

4. Install the oil screen and a new gasket.

   **Tightening torque**

   Oil screen bolt:

   15-22 Nm (150-220 kg.cm, 11-15 lb.ft)

5. Clean the gasket surfaces of the cylinder block and the oil pan.

6. Apply sealant to the groove of the oil pan flange.

> **NOTE**
> 1. *Make the first cut approx. 4 mm from the end of the nozzle furnished with the sealant. After application of the sealant, do not exceed 15 minutes before installing the oil pan.*
> 2. *Make sure the sealant doesn't enter the inside of the oil pan.*

7. Install the oil pan and tighten the bolts to the specified torque.

   **Tightening torque**

   Oil pan bolt:

   | Mark | Torque |
   |---|---|
   | * | 19-28 Nm (190-280 kg.cm, 14-20 lb.ft) |
   | ** | 5-7 Nm (50-70 kg.cm, 4-5 lb.ft) |
   | *** | 30-42 Nm (300-420 kg.cm, 22-30 lb.ft) |

<!-- Figure: Upper oil pan bolt tightening pattern (X-pattern), source: EMA.pdf page 58 -->

8. Tighten the oil pan bolts as shown in the illustration.

<!-- Figure: Lower oil pan bolt numbering sequence (bolts 1 through 17), source: EMA.pdf page 58 -->

9. Using a 24 mm deep socket, install the oil pressure switch after applying sealant to the threaded area.

   **Sealant:**
   Three bond No.1141E or 3M ATD No. 8660 or equivalent

> **NOTE**
> *Do not torque the oil pressure switch too much.*

   **Tightening torque**

   Oil pressure switch:

   15-22 Nm (150-220 kg.cm, 11-16 lb.ft)

<!-- Figure: Oil pressure switch installation with 24mm socket and sealant, source: EMA.pdf page 58 -->

10. Tighten the lower oil pan bolts as shown in figure.

<!-- Figure: Lower oil pan bolt tightening sequence, source: EMA.pdf page 58 -->
