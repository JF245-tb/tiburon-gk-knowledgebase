# Cooling System

## Engine Coolant Hose/Pipes

### Components <!-- EDHA6500 -->

<!-- Figure: Engine coolant hose/pipes exploded view showing radiator upper hose connector, radiator lower hose connector, thermostat, coolant temperature sensor, coolant inlet pipe, O-rings, heat hose connector, and gasket, source: EMA.pdf page 42 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Radiator upper hose connector | 17-20 Nm (170-200 kg.cm, 12-14 lb.ft) |
| Radiator lower hose connector | 17-20 Nm (170-200 kg.cm, 12-14 lb.ft) |
| Heat hose connector | 15-20 Nm (150-200 kg.cm, 11-14 lb.ft) |
| Coolant temperature sensor | 20-40 Nm (200-400 kg.cm, 14-29 lb.ft) |
| Coolant inlet pipe | 17-20 Nm (170-200 kg.cm, 12-14 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kg.cm, lb.ft)**

### Inspection <!-- EDHA6100 -->

Check the engine coolant pipe and hose for cracks, damage and restrictions. Replace if necessary.

### Reassembly <!-- EDUA0700 -->

Fit the O-ring in the groove of the engine coolant inlet pipe end. Wet the periphery of the O-ring with water and insert the engine coolant inlet pipe.

> **NOTE**
> 1. *Do not apply oil or grease to the engine coolant pipe O-ring.*
> 2. *Keep the engine coolant pipe connections free of sand, dust, etc.*
> 3. *Insert the engine coolant pipe into the end of the engine coolant pump inlet.*
> 4. *Whenever installing the engine coolant inlet pipe, always replace the O-ring with a new one.*

<!-- Figure: Coolant inlet pipe and O-ring installation at water pump, source: EMA.pdf page 43 -->

## Coolant Temperature Sensor

### Removal <!-- ECUA4800 -->

1. Drain the engine coolant.
2. Remove the engine harness after disconnecting the ground cable of the battery.
3. Remove the engine coolant sensor.

<!-- Figure: Coolant temperature sensor location on engine, source: EMA.pdf page 43 -->

### Installation <!-- EDHA6400 -->

1. Apply sealant to the sensor's threads. Tighten it to the specified torque.

   **Recommended sealant:**
   Three bond NO. 1324 or LOCTITE 262

   **Tightening torque**

   The coolant sensor:

   20-40 Nm (200-400 kg.cm, 14-29 lb.ft)

2. Connect the coolant sensor to the harness.
3. Connect the ground cable of battery.
4. Refill radiator with the coolant.

## Engine Coolant Pump

### Components <!-- EDHA7000 -->

<!-- Figure: Engine coolant pump exploded view showing cam shaft sprocket, tension bearing, auto tensioner, timing belt, crank shaft sprocket, idler bearing, water pump, and gasket, source: EMA.pdf page 45 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Cam shaft sprocket | 90-110 Nm (900-1100 kg.cm, 65-80 lb.ft) |
| Water pump bolts | 15-22 Nm (150-220 kg.cm) |
| Crank shaft sprocket | 20-27 Nm (200-270 kg.cm, 14-20 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kg.cm, lb.ft)**

### Water Pump Cross-Section <!-- EDHA7100 -->

<!-- Figure: Water pump cross-section showing water pump bearing assembly, seal unit, impeller, water pump pulley, water pump body, and gasket, source: EMA.pdf page 46 -->

### Removal <!-- EDHA7200 -->

1. Using the drain plug, drain the coolant.
2. Remove the drive belt and the engine coolant pump pulley.
3. Remove the timing belt cover, the auto tensioner and idler pulley.
4. Remove the engine coolant pump mounting bolts.
5. Remove the engine coolant pump assembly from the cylinder block.

### Inspection <!-- EDHA7300 -->

1. Check the engine coolant pump for cracks, damage or wear, and replace the pump assembly if necessary.
2. Check the bearing for damage, abnormal noise and sluggish rotation and replace the pump assembly if necessary.
3. Check the seal for leaks and replace the pump assembly if necessary.

### Installation <!-- EDOC1000 -->

1. Clean the gasket surfaces of the engine coolant pump body and the cylinder block.

<!-- Figure: Coolant inlet pipe, O-ring, and water pump installation, source: EMA.pdf page 47 -->

2. Install the new engine coolant pump gasket and pump assembly. Tighten the bolts to the specified torque.

   **Tightening torque**

   Engine coolant pump bolt

   Head mark "7":

   15-22 Nm (150-220 kg.cm, 11-16 lb.ft)

<!-- Figure: Water pump bolt head mark "7" detail, source: EMA.pdf page 47 -->

3. Install the auto tensioner and timing belt. Adjust the timing belt tension, then install the timing belt cover.
4. Install the drive belt, coolant pump pulley and then adjust the auto tensioner.
5. Refill the radiator with coolant.
6. Run the engine and check for leaks.

## Radiator

### Components <!-- ECOC1000 -->

<!-- Figure: Radiator assembly exploded view showing upper insulator, over flow tube, reservoir tank, radiator upper hose, radiator lower hose, lower insulator, oil cooler hose (A/T), and radiator fan assembly, source: EMA.pdf page 48 -->

### Removal <!-- ECOC1100 -->

1. Disconnect the ground cable from the battery terminal.
2. Disconnect the fan motor connector.
3. Loosen the radiator drain plug to drain the coolant.
4. Disconnect the upper and lower hoses and overflow tube after making marks on the radiator hose and the hose clamp.

<!-- Figure: Radiator mounting bolt locations in engine bay, source: EMA.pdf page 49 -->

5. For vehicles with automatic transaxles, disconnect the oil cooler hoses from the automatic transaxle.

> **CAUTION**
> *Cover or plug the hose and inlets of the radiator so that dust and other foreign materials can not enter after the hose is disconnected from the radiator.*

6. Remove the radiator upper mounting bolt.

<!-- Figure: Radiator upper mounting bolt removal, source: EMA.pdf page 49 -->

7. Remove the radiator together with the fan motor.
8. Remove the radiator fan motor and condenser fan motor from the radiator.

### Inspection <!-- ECUA9000 -->

1. Check for foreign material between the radiator fins.
2. Check the radiator fins for damage and straighten if necessary.
3. Check the radiator for corrosion, damage, rust or scale.
4. Check the radiator hoses for cracks, damage or deterioration.
5. Check the reservoir tank for damage.
6. Check the automatic transaxle oil cooler hoses for cracking, damage or deterioration (only A/T).

### Installation <!-- ECUA6200 -->

1. Fill the radiator and reservoir tank with a clean coolant mixture.
2. Run the engine until the coolant warms up enough so that the thermostat valve opens and then turn off the engine.
3. Remove the radiator cap and pour the coolant up to the filler neck of the radiator. Fill the reservoir tank to the upper level.
4. Check that there are no leaks from the radiator, hoses or connections.

## Radiator Fan Motor

### Components <!-- ECHA8200 -->

<!-- Figure: Radiator fan motor exploded view showing radiator, fan mounting clip, fan, shroud, and radiator fan motor, source: EMA.pdf page 50 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Shroud bolts | 8-10 Nm (80-100 kg.cm, 5.8-7 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kg.cm, lb.ft)**

### Removal <!-- ECUA8000 -->

1. Disconnect the ground cable from the battery cable.
2. Disconnect the connectors from the fan motor and the harness from the shroud.
3. For vehicles with automatic transaxles, remove the oil cooler hose from the shroud.
4. Remove the four bolts holding the shroud.

<!-- Figure: Shroud bolt locations, source: EMA.pdf page 50 -->

5. Remove the shroud with the fan motor.
6. Remove the fan mounting clip and detach the fan from the fan motor.
7. Remove the three screws and detach the fan motor.

### Inspection <!-- ECUA8800 -->

#### Radiator Fan Motor and Condenser Fan Motor

1. Check that the radiator fan rotates when battery voltage is applied between the terminals.
2. Check that there are no abnormal noises while the motor is running.

<!-- Figure: Fan motor terminal test setup with battery, source: EMA.pdf page 51 -->

#### Radiator Fan Motor Relay

1. Remove the radiator fan motor relay (High and Low) from the relay box in the engine room.

<!-- Figure: Relay box location in engine compartment, source: EMA.pdf page 51 -->

2. Check the continuity of the terminals.

| Current | Terminal No. | Yes or No |
|---|---|---|
| ON | Terminal 1-3 | Continuity |
| OFF | Terminal 1-3 | Non continuity |
| OFF | Terminal 2-4 | Continuity |

### Installation <!-- EDHA8900 -->

Installation is the reverse of removal.

> **NOTE**
> 1. *Make sure the cooling fan does not come into contact with the shroud when installed.*
> 2. *After installation, make sure there is no unusual noise or vibration when the fan is rotating.*

<!-- Figure: Fan motor installed in shroud assembly, source: EMA.pdf page 51 -->

## Radiator Cap

### Components <!-- ECHA6800 -->

<!-- Figure: Radiator cap exploded view showing radiator cap, spring, high pressure valve, bent valve, and vacuum spring, source: EMA.pdf page 52 -->

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| High pressure valve | 107.9 ± 14.7 kPa (1.1 ± 0.15 kg/cm², 15.64 ± 2.13 psi) |
| Vacuum spring | 83.4 kPa (0.85 kg/cm², 12.1 psi) |

**PRESSURE : kPa (kg/cm², psi)**

### Inspection <!-- EDHA6200 -->

1. Check the radiator cap for damage, cracks or weakening.

<!-- Figure: Radiator cap inspection points showing weakening areas, source: EMA.pdf page 52 -->

2. Connect the tester to the radiator cap.
3. Increase the pressure until the indicator of the gauge stops moving.
4. Replace the radiator cap if the reading does not hold steady for about 10 seconds.

## Thermostat

### Components <!-- EDHA6000 -->

<!-- Figure: Thermostat assembly exploded view showing coolant outlet fitting, gasket, coolant inlet fitting, thermostat, coolant temperature sensor, and coolant hose, source: EMA.pdf page 53 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Coolant outlet fitting | 17-20 Nm (170-200 kg.cm, 12-14 lb.ft) |
| Coolant inlet fitting | 17-20 Nm (170-200 kg.cm, 12-14 lb.ft) |
| Coolant hose connector | 15-20 Nm (150-200 kg.cm, 11-14 lb.ft) |
| Coolant temperature sensor | 20-40 Nm (200-400 kg.cm, 14-29 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kg.cm, lb.ft)**

### Removal <!-- EDHA8100 -->

1. Drain the coolant to thermostat level or below.
2. Remove the inlet fitting and gasket.
3. Remove the thermostat.

### Inspection <!-- EDHA6000 -->

1. Heat the thermostat as shown in the illustration.
2. Check that the valve operates properly.
3. Verify the temperature at which the valve begins to open.

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| Valve opening temperature | 80-84°C (176-183.2°F) |
| Full opening temperature | 95°C (203°F) |

<!-- Figure: Thermostat submerged in heated water with thermometer for valve opening test, source: EMA.pdf page 54 -->

### Installation <!-- EDHA6000 -->

1. Check that the flange of the thermostat is correctly placed in the socket of the thermostat housing.
2. Install the inlet fitting.

   **Tightening torque**

   Engine coolant inlet fitting bolt:

   17-20 Nm (170-200 kg.cm, 12-14 lb.ft)

3. Refill the coolant.

<!-- Figure: Thermostat installed in housing with inlet fitting, source: EMA.pdf page 54 -->
