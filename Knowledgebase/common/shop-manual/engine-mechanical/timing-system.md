# Timing System

## Timing Belt

### Components
<!-- Section code: EDHAC000 -->

<!-- Figure: Timing belt component layout showing engine support bracket, timing belt upper cover, timing belt lower cover, crank shaft pulley, and under cover panel, source: EMA.pdf page 75 -->

| Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Engine support bracket | 6–7 Nm (60–70 kg·cm, 4–5 lb·ft) |
| Timing belt upper cover | 15–22 Nm (150–220 kg·cm, 11–15 lb·ft) |
| Crank shaft pulley | 180–190 Nm (1,800–1,900 kg·cm, 130–138 lb·ft) |
| Timing belt lower cover | 10–12 Nm (100–120 kg·cm, 7–9 lb·ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kg·cm, lb·ft)**

### Removal
<!-- Section code: EDJA0600 -->

1. Remove the engine cover.

<!-- Figure: Engine cover removal, source: EMA.pdf page 76 -->

2. Using a [16 mm] wrench, rotate the tensioner arm clockwise (about 14°) and remove the belt from the pulley.
3. Remove the power steering pump pulley, idler pulley, tensioner pulley and crankshaft pulley.

<!-- Figure: Tensioner arm rotation and belt removal, source: EMA.pdf page 76 -->

4. Remove the upper and lower timing belt covers.

<!-- Figure: Timing belt cover removal, source: EMA.pdf page 76 -->

5. Remove the auto tensioner.

> **NOTE**
> *Rotate the crankshaft clockwise and align the timing mark to set the No.1 cylinder's piston to TDC (compression stroke). At this time, the timing marks of the camshaft sprocket and cylinder head cover should coincide with each other.*

<!-- Figure: Timing mark alignment on camshaft sprocket, source: EMA.pdf page 76 -->

6. Unbolt the tensioner to remove the timing belt.

> **NOTE**
> *If you plan to use the timing belt again, mark the rotation direction on the belt so you reinstall it correctly.*

<!-- Figure: Tensioner unbolt and timing belt removal, source: EMA.pdf page 76 -->

### Inspection
<!-- Section code: EDHAC200 -->

1. Inspect the belt closely. If the following problems are evident, replace the belt with a new one.

    1) Hardened back surface of rubber.
       Back surface is glossy, non-elastic and so hard that when the nail of your finger is pressed into it, no mark is produced.

    <!-- Figure: Hardened belt back surface, source: EMA.pdf page 77 -->

    2) Cracked back surface of rubber.

    <!-- Figure: Cracked belt showing crack and separation, source: EMA.pdf page 77 -->

    3) Side of belt is badly worn.

    > **NOTE**
    > *A belt in good condition should have clear-cut sides as if it were cut with a sharp knife.*

    <!-- Figure: Belt side wear showing separation, rounded belt side, and abnormal wear (fluffy canvas fiber), source: EMA.pdf page 77 -->

    4) Teeth are badly worn out.

       Initial stage: Canvas on load side of the tooth flank worn (fluffy canvas fibers, rubber gone, color changed to white, and unclear canvas texture).

       Last stage: Canvas on load side of the tooth flank worn down and rubber exposed (tooth width reduced).

    5) Missing tooth.

    <!-- Figure: Missing tooth showing flank worn on load side, rubber exposed, and tooth missing with canvas fiber exposed, source: EMA.pdf page 77 -->

2. If backlash or an irregular noise is observed when rotating the pulley, replace the timing belt tensioner and idler pulley.

<!-- Figure: Pulley rotation check, source: EMA.pdf page 77 -->

### Installation
<!-- Section code: EDJA0170 -->

1. Install the idler pulley to water pump boss.

> **NOTE**
> *Insert and install the idler pulley to the roll pin that is pressed in the water pump boss.*

<!-- Figure: Idler pulley installation on water pump boss, source: EMA.pdf page 78 -->

2. Install the tensioner arm and plain washer to the cylinder block.
3. Install the tensioner pulley to the tensioner arm.
4. Install the camshaft sprockets and align the timing marks.

<!-- Figure: Camshaft sprocket timing mark alignment, source: EMA.pdf page 79 -->

> **NOTE**
> - *When installing the camshaft sprockets, fasten them tightly while holding the hexagonal part of the camshaft.*
> - *Before installing the timing belt, if the timing marks of the cam sprocket and the cylinder head cover do not coincide, do not rotate the cam sprocket more than 3 teeth in either direction.*
> - *Rotating the sprocket more than 3 teeth may allow the valve and piston to touch each other.*
> - *If the cam sprocket is rotated more than 3 teeth unavoidably, rotate the crankshaft counter-clockwise a bit before rotating the cam sprocket.*

5. Install the auto tensioner to the front case.

> **NOTE**
> *At this time auto tensioner's set pin should be compressed and secured with set pin.*

<!-- Figure: Auto tensioner with set pin, source: EMA.pdf page 79 -->

6. Align the timing marks of each sprocket and install the timing belt in the following order:
   Crankshaft sprocket --> Idler pulley --> Camshaft sprocket (LH side) --> Water pump pulley --> Camshaft sprocket (RH side) --> Tensioner pulley.

> **NOTE**
> - *In this step, No. 1 cylinder is in the TDC (Compression stroke).*
> - *Be very careful if you use your fingers.*

<!-- Figure: Timing belt installation order on sprockets, source: EMA.pdf page 79 -->

7. Pull out the set pin of the auto tensioner.

<!-- Figure: Set pin removal from auto tensioner, source: EMA.pdf page 79 -->

8. Install the upper and lower timing belt covers.
9. Install the power steering pump pulley, idler pulley, tensioner pulley and crankshaft pulley.
10. Using the wrench [16mm], rotate the tensioner arm clockwise (about 14°) and install the belt to the pulley.
11. Install the engine cover.

## How to Adjust the Tension of the Timing Belt

1. Rotate the crankshaft 2 turns clockwise and measure the projected length of the auto tensioner at TDC (#1 Compression stroke) after 5 minutes.
2. The projected length should be 6–8 mm.
3. Verify that the timing marks of each sprocket are in their specified position.

> **NOTE**
> *If the timing marks are not in their specified position, repeat from 6 above.*
