# Intake and Exhaust System

## Exhaust Manifold

### Components
<!-- Section code: EDAC6600 -->

<!-- Figure: Exhaust manifold exploded view showing heat protectors, exhaust manifolds, gaskets, oxygen sensors, and muffler, source: EMA.pdf page 59 -->

| Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Oxygen sensor | 40–50 Nm (400–500 kg·cm, 29–36 lb·ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kg·cm, lb·ft)**

### Removal
<!-- Section code: EDOC1300 -->

1. Remove the heat protector.
2. Remove the exhaust manifold.
3. Remove the exhaust manifold gasket.

### Inspection
<!-- Section code: EDHA0206 -->

1. Check for damage or cracks.
2. Using a straight edge and feeler gauge, check for distortion on the cylinder head matching surface.

| Measurement | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard value | 0.15 mm (0.006 in.) or less |
| Service limit | 0.3 mm (0.012 in.) or less |

<!-- Figure: Straight edge and feeler gauge on exhaust manifold surface, source: EMA.pdf page 60 -->

3. Check the exhaust manifold for damage and cracks.

### Installation
<!-- Section code: EDOC1400 -->

1. Install the exhaust manifold with gasket.

**Tightening torque**

| Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Exhaust manifold | 25–30 Nm (250–300 kg·cm, 18–22 lb·ft) |

> **NOTE**
> *Do not re-use an exhaust manifold gasket.*

2. Install the heat protector.

<!-- Figure: Heat protector installation on engine, source: EMA.pdf page 60 -->

## Intake Manifold

### Components
<!-- Section code: EDHAA500 -->

<!-- Figure: Intake manifold exploded view showing surge tank, surge tank bracket, fuel pressure regulator, delivery pipe, injectors, gaskets, and intake manifold, source: EMA.pdf page 61 -->

| Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Surge tank bolts | 8–12 Nm (80–120 kg·cm, 6–9 lb·ft) |
| Surge tank bracket bolts | 15–20 Nm (150–200 kg·cm, 11–14 lb·ft) |
| Surge tank to intake manifold | 15–20 Nm (150–200 kg·cm, 11–14 lb·ft) |
| Intake manifold to cylinder head | 19–21 Nm (190–210 kg·cm, 14–15 lb·ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kg·cm, lb·ft)**

### Removal
<!-- Section code: EDHAA600 -->

1. Remove the air intake hose connected to the throttle body.

<!-- Figure: Air intake hose removal from throttle body, source: EMA.pdf page 62 -->

2. Remove the accelerator and cruise control cables.
3. Remove the engine coolant hose and throttle body.

<!-- Figure: Coolant hose and throttle body removal, source: EMA.pdf page 62 -->

4. Remove the P.C.V. hose and brake booster vacuum hoses.
5. Disconnect the vacuum hose connections.

<!-- Figure: Vacuum hose disconnection, source: EMA.pdf page 62 -->

6. Remove the surge tank stay.

<!-- Figure: Surge tank stay removal, source: EMA.pdf page 62 -->

7. Bleed off the pressure in the fuel pipe line to prevent the fuel from spilling.
8. Disconnect the connector from high pressure hose.

<!-- Figure: Fuel pipe line connector disconnection, source: EMA.pdf page 62 -->

9. Remove the surge tank and gasket.

<!-- Figure: Surge tank and gasket removal, source: EMA.pdf page 62 -->

10. Disconnect the fuel injector harness connector.

<!-- Figure: Fuel injector harness connector, source: EMA.pdf page 62 -->

11. Remove the delivery pipe with the fuel injector and the pressure regulator.

> **NOTE**
> *When the delivery pipe is removed, be careful not to drop an injector.*

<!-- Figure: Delivery pipe with fuel injectors and pressure regulator, source: EMA.pdf page 63 -->

12. Disconnect the wiring harness of the coolant sensor assembly.

<!-- Figure: Coolant sensor wiring harness disconnection, source: EMA.pdf page 63 -->

13. Remove the surge tank.

<!-- Figure: Surge tank removal from intake manifold, source: EMA.pdf page 63 -->

### Inspection
<!-- Section code: EDHAA100 -->

#### Surge Tank and Intake Manifold

1. Check the surge tank and intake manifold for damage, cracking or restriction of the vacuum outlet port, water or gas passages.
2. Check for distortion on the surface using a straight edge and feeler gauge.

| Measurement | Value |
|---|---|
| Standard value | 0.15 mm (0.006 in.) or less |
| Service limit | 0.2 mm (0.0078 in.) |

<!-- Figure: Straight edge measurement on intake manifold surface, source: EMA.pdf page 63 -->

### Installation
<!-- Section code: EDHAA800 -->

1. Install the intake manifold and delivery pipe reversing the order of the removal procedure.

**Tightening torque**

| Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Intake manifold | 19–21 Nm (190–210 kg·cm, 14–15 lb·ft) |
| Surge tank | 15–20 Nm (150–200 kg·cm, 11–14 lb·ft) |

<!-- Figure: Intake manifold installed on engine, source: EMA.pdf page 64 -->
<!-- Figure: Surge tank installed on engine, source: EMA.pdf page 64 -->

## Muffler

### Components
<!-- Section code: EDOC1600 -->

<!-- Figure: Exhaust system exploded view showing front exhaust pipe, catalytic converter, gasket, center pipe, center muffler, hanger, and main muffler, source: EMA.pdf page 65 -->

| Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Catalytic converter gasket joint | 30–40 Nm (300–400 kg·cm, 22–29 lb·ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kg·cm, lb·ft)**

### Removal

#### Main Muffler
<!-- Section code: EDOC1700 -->

> **CAUTION**
> *Before removing or inspecting the exhaust system, ensure that the exhaust system is cool.*

1. Disconnect the main muffler from the center exhaust pipe.
2. Remove the rubber hangers and the main muffler.

<!-- Figure: Main muffler and rubber hanger removal, source: EMA.pdf page 66 -->

#### Front Exhaust Pipe (Including Catalytic Converter)

1. Remove the front exhaust pipe from the center exhaust pipe.
2. Remove the front exhaust pipe bolt and the exhaust manifold pipe mounting nuts.

<!-- Figure: Front exhaust pipe bolt and manifold mounting nuts, source: EMA.pdf page 66 -->

3. Remove the front exhaust pipe.

### Inspection
<!-- Section code: EDHA0200 -->

1. Check the mufflers and pipes for leaks, corrosion and damage.
2. Check the rubber hangers for deterioration and cracks.

### Installation
<!-- Section code: EDHA0300 -->

1. Temporarily install the front exhaust pipe, the catalytic converter assembly, the center exhaust pipe and the main muffler, in this order.
2. Install the rubber hangers so that they hang equally left and right.
3. Tighten the parts securely and then confirm that there is no interference with any components.

## Air Cleaner (ACL)

### Components
<!-- Section code: EDJA0500 -->

<!-- Figure: Air cleaner exploded view showing air intake hose, air cleaner cover, air cleaner filter, clamps, resonator, and air cleaner body, source: EMA.pdf page 67 -->

**TORQUE : Nm (kg·cm, lb·ft)**

### Removal
<!-- Section code: EDJA0600 -->

1. Disconnect the air flow sensor connector.
2. Remove the air intake hose and air duct connected to the air cleaner.
3. Remove the three bolts attaching the air cleaner mounting brackets.
4. Detach the air cleaner.

<!-- Figure: Air cleaner mounting bracket bolt removal, source: EMA.pdf page 68 -->

5. Remove the air flow sensor from the air intake hose.

> **CAUTION**
> *Do not pull on the air flow sensor wires.*

### Inspection
<!-- Section code: EDJA0700 -->

1. Check the air intake hose, air cleaner cover for damage.
2. Check the air duct for damage.

<!-- Figure: Air duct inspection, source: EMA.pdf page 68 -->

3. Check the air cleaner element for restriction, contamination or damage. If the element is slightly restricted, remove dust and debris by blowing compressed air from the inside of the element. Replace the element if it cannot be cleaned.

<!-- Figure: Air cleaner element inspection, source: EMA.pdf page 68 -->

### Installation
<!-- Section code: EDHA0800 -->

1. Install the air cleaner assembly following the reverse order of removal.
