# Rear Suspension System

## Components <!-- SHCC2600 -->

<!-- Figure: Rear suspension system exploded view showing bracket, trailing arm, rear carrier assembly, rear crossmember assembly, reinforcement, rear suspension arm assembly with torque values: bracket 100-120 (1000-1200, 74-88), rear suspension arm mounting 160-180 (1500-1800, 118-133), rear crossmember 100-120 (1000-1200, 74-88), reinforcement bolts 100-120 (1000-1200, 74-88), and rear suspension arm assembly 160-180 (1500-1800, 118-133), source: SS.pdf page 20 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Bracket (trailing arm to body) | 100-120 Nm (1000-1200 kgf.cm, 74-88 lb.ft) |
| Rear suspension arm mounting | 160-180 Nm (1500-1800 kgf.cm, 118-133 lb.ft) |
| Rear crossmember mounting | 100-120 Nm (1000-1200 kgf.cm, 74-88 lb.ft) |
| Reinforcement bolts | 100-120 Nm (1000-1200 kgf.cm, 74-88 lb.ft) |
| Rear suspension arm assembly | 160-180 Nm (1500-1800 kgf.cm, 118-133 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft)**

### Removal <!-- SHVC2400 -->

1. Remove the wheel and tire.

2. Remove the stabilizer link.

<!-- Figure: Stabilizer link location on rear suspension, source: SS.pdf page 20 -->

<!-- SS-21 -->

3. After loosening the rear trailing arm mounting bolts, remove the trailing arm.

4. Remove the rear suspension arm mounting bolt from the rear axle carrier.

<!-- Figure: Rear suspension arm and trailing arm removal showing bolt locations, source: SS.pdf page 21 -->

5. After supporting the center of the rear crossmember assembly with a jack, remove the rear crossmember mounting bolts to the body.

6. Remove the rear crossmember and suspension arm.

### Inspection <!-- SPHC2500 -->

1. Check the rubber parts for damage or deterioration.
2. Check the trailing arm and suspension arm for bent or deterioration.
3. Check the bolts for damage or rust.

### Installation <!-- SHVC2600 -->

1. Installation is the reverse of removal.

2. Tighten the components below to the specified torque as follows:

| Items | Torque Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Trailing arm mounting | 100-120 (1000-1200, 74-88) |
| Trailing arm bracket mounting | 40-50 (400-500, 29-37) |
| Stabilizer bar bracket | 17-26 (170-260, 13-19) |
| Stabilizer link nut | 34-45 (340-450, 13-33) |
| Front/Rear suspension arm mounting | 160-180 (1500-1800, 118-133) |
| Rear crossmember mounting | 100-130 (1000-1200, 74-88) |

* Should be temporarily tightened, and then fully tightened with the vehicle on the ground in an unladen condition.

## Rear Strut

### Components <!-- SHMC2100 -->

<!-- Figure: Rear strut assembly exploded view showing rear coil spring, self-locking nut 40-50 (400-500, 29-37), pipe, strut insulator, dust cover, spring lower pad, and strut-to-body mounting 110-130 (1100-1300, 81-96) with torque callout 30-40 (300-400, 22-29), source: SS.pdf page 22 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Self-locking nut | 40-50 Nm (400-500 kgf.cm, 29-37 lb.ft) |
| Strut upper mounting nut | 30-40 Nm (300-400 kgf.cm, 22-29 lb.ft) |
| Strut to carrier mounting | 110-130 Nm (1100-1300 kgf.cm, 81-96 lb.ft) |

> **CAUTION**
> * Replace the self-locking nuts with new ones after removal.

**TORQUE : Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft)**

<!-- SS-23 -->
### Removal <!-- SHVC2200 -->

1. Remove the covering self side trim, rear seat belt retractor, luggage side trim and covering self mounting bracket.

<!-- Figure: Rear seat belt retractor, covering self mounting bracket, luggage side trim, and covering self side trim removal sequence, source: SS.pdf page 23 -->

2. Remove the rear strut upper mounting nuts (3).

<!-- Figure: Three upper mounting nuts accessible from inside trunk, source: SS.pdf page 23 -->

3. Remove the wheel and tire.

4. Disconnect the brake hose and wheel speed sensor wiring from the rear strut.

5. Remove the stabilizer bar link.

<!-- Figure: Stabilizer bar link at rear strut, source: SS.pdf page 23 -->

6. Remove the strut and carrier mounting bolts(2).

> **CAUTION**
> Be careful not to drop the rear strut.

7. Remove the rear strut assembly.

### Disassembly <!-- SHVC2300 -->

1. Using the Special Tools (09546-26000 or J38402), compress the coil spring until there is only a little tension on the strut.

> **NOTE**
> Do not use an impact gun.

<!-- Figure: Strut spring compressor tool with adaptors A-20, A-00 and tool J38402, source: SS.pdf page 23 -->

2. Remove the self-locking nut at the top of the shock absorber.

3. Remove the insulator, coil spring and dust cover from strut assembly.

### Inspection <!-- SYHKA71 -->

1. Check the strut insulator for wear or damage.
2. Check rubber parts for damage or deterioration.
3. Check the coil spring and strut assembly for sagging and deformation.
4. Check the shock absorber for abnormal resistance or unusual sound.

<!-- Figure: Shock absorber inspection, source: SS.pdf page 23 -->

<!-- SS-24 -->
### Reassembly <!-- BHCC2000 -->

1. Install the lower spring pad so that the protrusions fit in the hole in the spring lower seat.

<!-- Figure: Spring lower seat with protrusions (three places) fitting into holes (three places), source: SS.pdf page 24 -->

2. Install the dust cover on the shock absorber.

3. Using the Special Tools (09546-26000 or J38402), compress the coil spring.

<!-- Figure: Coil spring being compressed with special tools J38402 with adaptors A-20 and A-00, and tool 09546-26000, source: SS.pdf page 24 -->

4. After extending the piston rod fully, install the insulator assembly and pipe.

> **NOTE**
> Align the D-shaped hole in the spring seat upper assembly with the protrusion of the piston rod.

<!-- Figure: Coil spring, fitting, and rear strut insulator assembly alignment, source: SS.pdf page 24 -->

5. After seating the lower ends of the coil spring in the lower spring seat grooves correctly, tighten the new self-locking nut temporarily.

> **CAUTION**
> Replace the self-locking nut with new ones after removal.

<!-- Figure: Coil spring edge seated in spring seat groove, source: SS.pdf page 24 -->

6. Remove the Special Tools (09546-26000 or J38402).

7. Tighten the self-locking nut to the specified torque.

**Tightening torque**

40-55 Nm (400-550 kgf.cm, 29.6-40.7 lb.ft)

## Trailing Arm

### Removal <!-- EHKB0130 -->

1. Detach the parking brake cable from the rear trailing arm bracket.

2. After loosening the rear trailing arm mounting bolts, remove the trailing arm.

<!-- Figure: Trailing arm mounting bolt removal, source: SS.pdf page 25 -->

3. Remove the rear suspension arm mounting bolt from the rear axle carrier.

<!-- Figure: Rear suspension arm and trailing arm removal from carrier, source: SS.pdf page 25 -->

4. After supporting the center of the rear crossmember assembly with a jack, remove the rear crossmember mounting bolts to the body.

5. Remove the rear crossmember and suspension arm.

> **NOTE**

## Rear Stabilizer Bar

### Removal <!-- BHKC2200 -->

1. Remove the stabilizer bar link from the rear strut assembly.

2. Remove the rear stabilizer bar mounting brackets.

3. Remove the stabilizer bar.

### Inspection <!-- SYHDA924 -->

Check the stabilizer link ball joint rotating torque.

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09532-11600 measuring rear stabilizer link ball joint rotating torque, source: SS.pdf page 27 -->

1. If there is a crack in the dust cover, replace it and add grease.
2. Shake the stabilizer link ball joint stud several times.
3. Mount the self-locking nut on the ball joint, and then measure the ball joint rotating torque.

**Standard value**

1.7-3.2 Nm (17-32 kgf.cm, 15-27 lb.in.)

4. If the rotating torque is above the upper limit of the standard value, replace the stabilizer link.
5. If the rotating torque is below the lower limit of the standard value, the ball joint may be reused unless it has drag and excessive play.

### Installation <!-- BHKC2300 -->

1. Install the bushing on the stabilizer bar.

> **NOTE**
> a. When installing the stabilizer bar, follow the identification color (I.D color) as below:
>    I.D color : Green
>    Outer diameter : 19mm (0.75 in.)
>
> b. After matching the bushing in the inside of the white painted part on the stabilizer bar, install them.

<!-- Figure: Stabilizer bar bushing installation showing I.D color, white paint mark, and bushing position, source: SS.pdf page 27 -->

2. Install the bracket on the bushing.

3. Tighten the components below to the specified torque as follows:

Rear stabilizer bar mounting bracket :
17-26 Nm (170-260 kgf.cm, 13-19 lb.ft)

Rear stabilizer bar link mounting :
35-45 Nm (350-450 kgf.cm, 26-33 lb.ft)

# Tires/Wheels

## Tire

### Front Wheel Alignment <!-- SHVC3700 -->

When using a wheel alignment tester to inspect the front wheel alignment, always position the car on a level surface with the front wheels facing straight ahead. Prior to inspection, make sure that the front suspension and steering system are in normal operating conditions and that the wheels and tires face straight ahead and the tires are inflated to the specified pressures.

**TOE-IN**

Toe-in (B-A or angle a+B) is adjusted by turning the tie rod turnbuckle. Toe-in on the left front wheel can be reduced by turning the tie rod toward the rear of the car. Total change is adjusted by turning the tie rods for the right and left wheels simultaneously at the same amount as follows:

**Standard value**

Toe-in(B-A) mm (in.) : 0 x 2 mm (0 x 0.08 in.)

<!-- Figure: Front wheel toe-in measurement diagram showing forward direction and measurement points A and B, source: SS.pdf page 28 -->

> **NOTE**
> - Toe-in adjustment should be made by turning the right and left tie rods at the same amount.
> - When adjusting turn, loosen the outer bellows clip to prevent twisting the bellows.
> - After the adjustment, tighten the tie rod and lock nut firmly and reinstall the bellows clip.
> - Adjust each toe-in to be in the range of x 1mm.

**Tightening torque**

Tie rod and lock nuts :
50-55 Nm (500-550 kgf.cm, 37-41 lb.ft)

**CAMBER**

The steering knuckle which is installed with the strut assembly is pre-set to the specified camber at the factory and doesn't need to be adjusted.

Camber : 0°13' x 30'

**CASTER**

Caster is pre-set at the factory and doesn't need to be adjusted. If the caster is not within the standard value, replace the bent or damaged parts.

Caster : 2°58' x 30'

<!-- Figure: Caster angle diagram showing forward direction with C5 measurement, source: SS.pdf page 28 -->

> **NOTE**
> 1. The worn loose or damaged parts of the front suspension assembly must be replaced prior to measuring front wheel alignment.
> 2. Measure the wheel alignment by using special tool (09526-21000).
> 3. Camber and caster are pre-set to the specified value at the factory and don't need to be adjusted.
> 4. If the camber and caster are not within specification, replace the bent or damaged parts.
> 5. The difference of left and right wheels for the camber and the caster must be within the range of 0° x 30'.

### Rear Wheel Alignment

**TOE-IN**

Standard value : -4'7 mm (0.1632 in.)

<!-- Figure: Rear wheel toe-in measurement diagram, source: SS.pdf page 29 -->

> **NOTE**
> - Adjust the toe-in by turning the tie rod end of the rear suspension arm.
> - Left tie rod : Clockwise direction : toe-in
> - Right tie rod : Clockwise direction : toe-out
>
> A variation of toe by a rotation of the tie rod :
> - About 6mm (0.6°)
>
> - The cam bolt should be adjusted to a maximum of 40° left or right from the center position.

> **CAUTION**
> After adjusting the tie rod, tighten both nuts to the specified torque.

**Specified torque**

50-60 Nm (500-600 kgf.cm, 37-43 lb.ft)

<!-- Figure: Rear suspension arm tie rod adjustment showing 50-60 Nm (500-600 kgf.cm, 37-43 lb.ft) torque callout, source: SS.pdf page 29 -->

### Tire Wear

1. Measure the tread depth of the tires.

Tread depth of tire (Limit) : 1.6 mm (0.06 in.)

<!-- Figure: Tread depth measurement with gauge tool 09529-21000, source: SS.pdf page 29 -->

2. If the remaining tread depth is less than the limit, replace the tire.

> **NOTE**
> When the tread depth of the tires is less than 1.6 mm (0.06 in.) the wear indicators will appear.

<!-- Figure: Tire wear indicator diagram, source: SS.pdf page 29 -->

## Wheel

### Wheel Runout <!-- EHCC3600 -->

1. Jack up the vehicle and support it with jack stands.

2. Measure the wheel runout with a dial indicator as illustrated.

<!-- Figure: Dial indicator measuring wheel runout, source: SS.pdf page 30 -->

3. Replace the wheel if the wheel runout exceeds the limit.

**Wheel runout (Limit)**

Steel wheel :
Radial 0.6 mm (0.028 in.)
(Average of LH & RH)
Axial 1.0 mm (0.039 in.)

Aluminum wheel :
Radial 0.3 mm (0.012 in.)
Axial 0.3 mm (0.012 in.)

### Tightening Wheel Nut

1. Tightening torque
   Steel and aluminum alloy wheel

**Specified torque**

90-110 Nm (900-1,100 kgf.cm, 65-80 lb.ft)

> **CAUTION**
> When using an impact gun, final tightening torque should be checked using a torque wrench.

2. Tightening order
   Check the torque again after tightening the wheel nuts diagonally.

<!-- Figure: 5-bolt wheel nut tightening sequence diagram showing order 1-2-3-4-5 in star pattern, source: SS.pdf page 30 -->

### Wheel Rotation <!-- BHCC3800 -->

Rotate the tires in the pattern illustrated.

> **CAUTION**
> When rotating the 215/45 ZR17 tires, ensure to follow the "ROTATION" direction marked on the sidewall of tires.
> When rotating the tires of the left and right, separate the wheel from the tire and then re-assemble them.

<!-- Figure: Tire rotation pattern diagram showing 4-tire cross rotation (front-to-rear, rear-to-opposite-front), and directional tire notation showing sidewall and tire rotation direction, source: SS.pdf page 30 -->

### Checking for Pull and Wander

1. If the steering pulls to one side, rotate the tires according to the following wheel rotation procedure.

a. Rotate the front right and front left tires, and perform a road test in order to confirm vehicle stability.

<!-- Figure: Tire rotation diagram showing front (F) and rear (R) positions with arrows indicating front left-right swap, source: SS.pdf page 30 -->

<!-- SS-31 -->

b. If the steering pulls to the opposite side, rotate the front and rear tires, and perform a road test again.

<!-- Figure: Tire rotation diagram showing front-to-rear and rear-to-front swap on both sides, source: SS.pdf page 31 -->

c. If the steering continues to pull to one side, rotate the front right and left tires again, and perform a road test.

<!-- Figure: Tire rotation diagram showing front left-right swap again, source: SS.pdf page 31 -->

d. If the steering continues to pull to the opposite side, replace the front wheels with new ones.

<!-- Figure: Tire rotation diagram showing new wheel replacement at front positions, source: SS.pdf page 31 -->
