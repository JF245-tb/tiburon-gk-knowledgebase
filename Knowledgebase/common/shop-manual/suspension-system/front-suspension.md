# Front Suspension System

## Strut Assembly

### Components <!-- SHCC0700 -->

<!-- Figure: Front strut assembly exploded view showing self-locking nut 50-70 (500-700, 37-51), strut insulator, Macpherson strut bearing, spring upper seat, spring upper pad, coil spring, dust cover, bumper rubber, strut assembly 140-160 (1400-1600, 104-118), and upper mounting nut 40-50 (400-500, 29-37), source: SS.pdf page 10 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Self-locking nut | 50-70 Nm (500-700 kgf.cm, 37-51 lb.ft) |
| Strut upper mounting nut | 40-50 Nm (400-500 kgf.cm, 29-37 lb.ft) |
| Strut assembly to knuckle | 140-160 Nm (1400-1600 kgf.cm, 104-118 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft)**

<!-- SS-11 -->
### Removal <!-- SHVC0800 -->

1. Remove the front wheel.

2. Detach the brake hose bracket from the strut assembly.

> **NOTE**
> Do not apply excessive force to the components.

3. Remove the stabilizer link.

4. Remove the strut upper mounting bolts(3).

<!-- Figure: Strut upper mounting bolts removal, source: SS.pdf page 11 -->

5. Remove the strut assembly.

<!-- Figure: Strut assembly removal from vehicle, source: SS.pdf page 11 -->

### Installation <!-- SHVC0900 -->

1. When installing the front strut, be sure to clear the connecting surface.

2. Install the strut assembly so the identification label on the strut insulator faces toward the inside of vehicle.

<!-- Figure: Strut insulator identification label orientation showing inner/outer positioning, source: SS.pdf page 11 -->

3. Tighten the components below to the specified torque as follows:

| Items | Torque Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Front strut upper mounting nut | 40-50 (400-500, 29-37) |
| Front strut to knuckle | 140-160 (1400-1600, 104-118) |
| Stabilizer link nut | 35-45 (350-450, 26-33) |

4. Install the brake hose and front wheel speed sensor wire on the front strut assembly.

### Disassembly <!-- SHVC1000 -->

1. Using the Special Tools (09546-26000 or J38402), compress the coil spring until there is only a little tension on the strut.

> **NOTE**
> Do not use an impact gun.

<!-- Figure: Strut spring compressor 09546-26000 in use with adaptors A-42, source: SS.pdf page 11 -->

2. Remove the nut at the top end of shock absorber.

<!-- SS-12 -->

3. Remove the insulator, spring seat, coil spring, dust cover from the strut assembly.

### Inspection <!-- SHCCA100 -->

1. Check the strut insulator bearing for wear or damage.
2. Check rubber parts for damage or deterioration.
3. Check the coil spring for sagging and weakness.
4. Check the shock absorber for abnormal resistance or unusual sound.

<!-- Figure: Shock absorber inspection illustration, source: SS.pdf page 12 -->

### Disposal <!-- EHCC0100 -->

1. Fully extend the shock absorber rod.
2. Drill a hole on the A section to remove gas from the cylinder.

> **CAUTION**
> The gas coming out is harmless, but be careful of chips that may fly when drilling. Be sure to wear face shield and safety goggles.

<!-- Figure: Shock absorber disposal showing drill point at section A and gas release, source: SS.pdf page 12 -->

### Reassembly <!-- SHVC1100 -->

1. Install lower spring pad so that the protrusions fit in the holes of the spring lower seat.

<!-- Figure: Spring lower seat showing protrusions (three places) fitting into holes (three places), source: SS.pdf page 12 -->

2. Install the dust cover on the shock absorber.

3. Using the Special Tools (09546-26000 or J38402), compress the coil spring.

4. After fully extending the piston rod, install the spring upper seat and insulator assembly.

> **NOTE**
> Align the D-shaped hole in the spring seat upper assembly with the protrusion on the piston rod.

<!-- Figure: Upper spring pad and upper spring seat alignment showing D-shaped hole and protrusion, source: SS.pdf page 12 -->

<!-- SS-13 -->

5. After seating the upper and lower ends of the coil spring in the upper and lower spring seat grooves correctly, tighten the new self-locking nut temporarily.

<!-- Figure: Coil spring edge seated in spring seat groove, source: SS.pdf page 13 -->

6. Remove the Special Tool (09546-26000 or J38402).

7. Tighten the self-locking nut to the specified torque.

**Tightening torque**

50-70 Nm (500-700 kgf.cm, 37-51 lb.ft)

## Lower Arm

### Components <!-- BHCC1400 -->

<!-- Figure: Lower arm assembly exploded view showing stabilizer bar link 30-45 (300-450, 22-33), bracket 24-34 (240-340, 19-25), bushing, stabilizer bar, strut assembly 140-160 (1400-1600, 104-118), sub frame, lower arm assembly 35-45 (300-450, 22-33), and mounting bolts 130-150 (1300-1500, 96-110), source: SS.pdf page 14 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Stabilizer bar link | 30-45 Nm (300-450 kgf.cm, 22-33 lb.ft) |
| Bracket | 24-34 Nm (240-340 kgf.cm, 19-25 lb.ft) |
| Strut assembly to knuckle | 140-160 Nm (1400-1600 kgf.cm, 104-118 lb.ft) |
| Lower arm assembly bolt | 35-45 Nm (300-450 kgf.cm, 22-33 lb.ft) |
| Lower arm bushing mounting bolt | 130-150 Nm (1300-1500 kgf.cm, 96-110 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft)**

<!-- SS-15 -->
### Removal <!-- SHVC1500 -->

1. Remove the front wheel.

2. Remove the driveshaft split pin, nut and washer.

3. Loosen the lower arm ball joint nut, but do not remove it.

4. Remove the strut lower mounting bolts(2).

5. Push the axle hub toward the outside to disconnect the driveshaft from the axle hub.

6. Using the Special Tool (09568 - 34000), disconnect the lower arm ball joint from the lower arm.

<!-- Figure: Ball joint puller 09568-34000 separating ball joint from knuckle, source: SS.pdf page 15 -->

7. Temporary install the strut lower mounting bolt.

8. Remove the lower arm bushing (A) and bushing (G) mounting bolts(2).

9. Remove the lower arm assembly.

### Installation <!-- SHVC1600 -->

Installation is the reverse of removal.

> **NOTE**
> Tighten the components below to the specified torque as follows:

| Items | Torque Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Wheel nut | 90-110 (900-1100, 67-82) |
| Driveshaft nut | |
| 1.6,2.0 L | 200-260 (2000-2600, 148-192) |
| 2.7 L | 200-280 (2000-2800, 148-207) |
| Strut lower mounting bolt | 140-160 (1400-1600, 104-118) |
| Lower arm ball joint nut | 60-72 (600-730, 43-52) |
| Lower arm bushing (A) | 130-150 (1300-1500, 96-111) |
| Lower arm bushing (G) | 130-150 (1300-1500, 96-111) |
| Stabilizer link nut | 35-45 (300-450, 29-33) |

### Replacement — Ball Joint and Dust Cover <!-- SHVC1700 -->

1. Using a flat-tipped screwdriver, remove the dust cover from the lower arm ball joint.

<!-- Figure: Dust cover removal with flat-tipped screwdriver, source: SS.pdf page 15 -->

2. Remove the snap ring.

3. Using a plastic hammer, tap the ball joint out of the lower arm.

<!-- SS-16 -->

4. Using Special Tool (09545 - 11000), press-fit the ball joint into the lower arm assembly.

<!-- Figure: Special tools 09545-02000, 09216-21200, 09216-21100 positioned for ball joint press-fit, with parts 09545-11000 (A) and (B), source: SS.pdf page 16 -->

5. Install the snap ring.

6. Using the Special Tool (09545 - 21100), install the dust cover.

<!-- Figure: Dust cover installer 09545-21100 in use, source: SS.pdf page 16 -->

### Lower Arm Bushing (G) <!-- EHKC1900 -->

1. Install the Special Tools (09545-02000, 09216-21100 and 09216-21200) on the lower arm.

2. Press out the bushing.

3. Apply soap solution to the following parts:
   - Outer surface of the bushing
   - Inner surface of the lower arm bushing mounting part

4. Install the new bushing on the lower arm by using Special Tools (09216 - 21100, 09624 - 34000).

<!-- Figure: Special tools 09216-21100 and 09624-34000 pressing bushing into lower arm, source: SS.pdf page 16 -->

> **NOTE**
> Press in the lower arm bushing (G) in the same direction as shown in illustration.

**Pull out force for the bushing:**
80 N (800 kgf), 11.9 lb(f) or more

<!-- Figure: Lower arm bushing (G) cross-section showing 13.1 +/- 0.5mm dimension at Section A-A with 60 degree angle, source: SS.pdf page 16 -->

<!-- SS-17 -->
### Inspection <!-- EHNC1800 -->

1. Check the bushing for wear and deterioration.
2. Check the lower arm for bending or breakage.
3. Check the ball joint dust cover for cracks and damage.
4. Check all bolts for damage and deformation.
5. Check the lower arm ball joint for rotating torque.

<!-- Figure: Torque wrench and special tool 09532-11600 measuring ball joint rotating torque, source: SS.pdf page 17 -->

- If there is a crack in the dust cover, replace the ball joint assembly.
- Shake the ball joint stud several times.
- Measure the ball joint rotating torque.

**Standard value**

3.5-10 Nm (35-100 kgf.cm, 2.6-7.4 lb.in)

- If the rotating torque is above the upper limit of the standard value, replace the ball joint assembly.
- Even if the rotating torque is below the lower limit of the standard value, the ball joint may be reused unless it has drag and excessive play.

## Front Stabilizer Bar

### Components <!-- EHCC1900 -->

<!-- Figure: Front stabilizer bar assembly showing stabilizer bar, bracket with bushing, and stabilizer link with torque values 30-45 (300-450, 22-33) at both mounting points, source: SS.pdf page 18 -->

| Fastener / Component | Torque |
|---|---|
| Stabilizer link nut | 30-45 Nm (300-450 kgf.cm, 22-33 lb.ft) |
| Stabilizer bracket bolt | 30-45 Nm (300-450 kgf.cm, 22-33 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE : Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft)**

### Removal <!-- SHVC2000 -->

1. Remove the front wheel.

2. Remove the stabilizer link assembly.

<!-- Figure: Stabilizer link removal, source: SS.pdf page 18 -->

3. Remove the stabilizer bracket and bushing

<!-- SS-19 -->

4. Remove the stabilizer bar.

### Inspection <!-- SPHC2100 -->

1. Check the stabilizer bar for deterioration and damage.
2. Check all bolts for damage and deformation.
3. Check the stabilizer link dust cover for crack or damage.
4. Check the stabilizer link ball joint for rotating torque.

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09532-11600 measuring stabilizer link ball joint rotating torque, source: SS.pdf page 19 -->

- If there is a crack in the dust cover, replace it and add grease.
- Shake the stabilizer link ball joint stud several times.
- Mount the self-locking nut on the ball joint, and then measure the ball joint rotating torque.

**Standard value**

0.7-2 Nm (7-20 kgf.cm, 6-18 lb.in.)

- If the rotating torque is higher than the upper limit of the standard value, replace the stabilizer link.
- If the rotating torque is below the lower limit of the standard value, the ball joint may be reused unless it has drag and excessive play.

### Installation <!-- SHVC2200 -->

1. Install the bushing on the stabilizer bar.

> **NOTE**
> a. When installing the stabilizer bar, follow the identification color (I.D color) as below:
>
> | Model | I.D color | Outer diameter |
> |-------|-----------|----------------|
> | 1.6L,2.0L | Yellow | 23mm (0.91 in.) |
> | 2.7L | Yellow | 23mm (0.91 in.) |
>
> b. Position the bushing on the outside of the stabilizer bar clamp so as to install it.

<!-- Figure: Stabilizer bar bushing installation showing I.D color marking, bushing, stabilizer bar, and clamp positions, source: SS.pdf page 19 -->

2. Install the bracket on the bushing.

3. After tightening the bolts of the bushing bracket temporarily, install the bushing bracket on the opposite side.
