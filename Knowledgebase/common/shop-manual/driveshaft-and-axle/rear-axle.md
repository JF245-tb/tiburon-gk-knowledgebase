# Front Axle

## Front Hub/Knuckle <!-- DS-18 -->

### Components

<!-- Figure: Exploded view showing:
- Knuckle
- Hub
- Brake disc
- Snap ring
- Wheel bearing
- Dust cover

Torque values:
- 1.6L, 2.0L: 200-260 (2000-2600, 148-192)
- 2.7L: 200-280 (2000-2800, 148-207)
-->

**TORQUE: Nm (kgf-cm, lb-ft)**

### Removal <!-- DS-18 -->

1. Remove the front wheel.
2. Remove the split pin and driveshaft nut from the front hub.
3. Remove the front brake assembly from the knuckle and suspend it with a wire.
4. Remove the vehicle speed sensor from the knuckle.
5. Disconnect the tie rod and ball joint from the knuckle by using the Special Tool (09568-34000).

> **NOTE**
> Be sure to tie the special tool (09568-34000) to the near part with cord not to fall.

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09568-34000 ball joint puller in use -->

6. Disconnect the strut assembly from the knuckle.
7. Disconnect the driveshaft from the hub.

<!-- DS-19 -->

8. Disconnect the lower arm ball joint from the knuckle by using the Special Tool (09568-34000).
9. Remove the hub and knuckle as an assembly.

### Installation <!-- DS-19 -->

1. Installation is the reverse of removal.

> **NOTE**
> Tighten the components below to the specified torque as follows:

| Items | Torque Nm (kgf-cm, lb-ft) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Driveshaft nut (1.6L, 2.0L) | 200–260 (2000–2600, 148–192) |
| Driveshaft nut (2.7L) | 200–280 (2000–2800, 148–207) |
| Lower arm ball joint to knuckle nut | 60–72 (600–720, 44–53) |
| Knuckle to strut assembly | 115–130 (1100–1300, 81–96) |

2. Install the washer behind the driveshaft nut with the convex side outward as shown in the illustration.

<!-- Figure: Driveshaft nut, split pin, washer installation detail -->

### Disassembly <!-- DS-19 -->

1. After removing the screws (2) mounting the brake disc, remove the brake disc from the hub.
2. Remove the snap ring.
3. Install the Special Tools as illustrated:
   - 09517-21500
   - 09517-29000

<!-- Figure: Special tools 09517-21500 and 09517-29000 installed on knuckle -->

4. Remove the hub from the knuckle by turning the Special Tool.

<!-- DS-20 -->

5. Remove the Special Tool and dust cover.
6. Remove the bearing inner race from the hub by using the Special Tool.

   Tools: 09495-33000

7. Using the Special Tool (09495-33100, 09517-29000), remove the wheel bearing outer race from the knuckle.

   Tools: 09495-33100, 09517-29000

### Inspection <!-- DS-20 -->

1. Check the hub for cracks and the splines for wear.
2. Check the brake disc for scoring and damage.
3. Check the knuckle for cracks.
4. Check the bearing for cracks or damage.

### Reassembly <!-- DS-20 -->

1. Apply multi-purpose grease to the contacting surface of the knuckle hub and bearing firmly.
2. Using the Special Tool (09495-33100, 09517-29000), press the bearing to the knuckle.

> **NOTE**
> 1. Press the outer race of the wheel bearing to prevent damage to the bearing assembly.
> 2. When installing a bearing assembly, always use a new one.

   Tools: 09532-11500

3. Install the dust cover.
4. Using the Special Tool (09495-33100), press-in the hub to the knuckle.

> **NOTE**
> Press in the inner race of the wheel bearing to prevent damage to bearing assembly.

   Tools: 09495-33100

<!-- DS-21 -->

5. Install the brake disc.
6. Tighten the hub and install the knuckle to the specified torque by using the Special Tool (09517-21500).

   Specified torque Nm (kgf-cm, lb-ft):
   - 1.6L, 2.0L: 200–260 (2000–2600, 148–192)
   - 2.7L: 200–280 (2000–2800, 148–207)

7. Rotate the hub several times to seat the bearing.
8. Measure the hub bearing starting torque.

   Hub bearing starting torque [Limit]:
   - 1.8 Nm (18 kgf-cm, 16 lb-in.) or less

   Tools: 09517-21500, 09532-11600

9. If the starting torque is 0 Nm (0 kgf-cm, 0 lb-in.), measure the hub bearing axial play.
10. If the hub axial play exceeds the limit while the nut is tightened to 200–280 Nm (2000–2800 kgf-cm, 148–192 lb-ft), the bearing, hub and knuckle are not installed correctly. Repeat the disassembly and assembly procedure.

    Hub bearing axial play [Limit]:
    - 0.008 mm (0.0003 in.) or less

11. Remove the Special Tool (09517-21500).

# Rear Axle

## Rear Axle/Hub <!-- DS-22 -->

### Components

<!-- Figure: Exploded view showing:
- Speed sensor
- Tone wheel
- Caliper assembly
- Hub assembly
- Brake disc
- Torque washer
- Flange nut

Torque values:
- 6.5-7.5 — Speed sensor bolt
- 200-260 (2000-2600, 148-192) — Flange nut
-->

**TORQUE: Nm (kgf-cm, lb-ft)**

### Removal <!-- DS-22 -->

1. Remove the rear wheel speed sensor (for vehicles equipped with ABS).

> **CAUTION**
> When removing the speed sensor from the adapter, be careful so that the end of the pole piece doesn't strike teeth on the rotor or other components.

2. Remove the caliper assembly and suspend it with a wire.
3. Remove the brake disc.
4. Remove the hub cap, wheel bearing nut and torque washer.
5. Remove the hub assembly.

### Inspection <!-- DS-23 -->

1. Check the oil seal for cracks or damage.
2. Check the rear hub bearing for wear or damage.
3. Check the rotor for chipped teeth.
4. Check the rear carrier for cracks.

### Installation <!-- DS-23 -->

1. After tightening the flange nut, caulk the concave portion of the spindle by crimping the nut.

> **CAUTION**
> Replace flange nut with new ones after removal.

<!-- Figure: Spindle concave portion caulking illustration -->

2. Installation of the rear speed sensor (for vehicles equipped with ABS): Insert the pole piece into the space between the speed sensors and the rotor teeth's surface, and then tighten the speed sensor at the position where the clearance at all places is within the standard value.

   Standard value:
   - Clearance: 0.2–1.3 mm (0.008–0.051 in.)

<!-- Figure: Rear speed sensor clearance measurement illustration -->

3. Install the hub cap.

> **CAUTION**
> The rear hub assembly should not be disassembled. (For vehicles equipped with ABS) Care must be taken not to scratch or damage the teeth of the rotor. The rotor must never be dropped. If the teeth of the rotor are chipped, it results in deterioration of the rotor. It will make it impossible to detect the wheel rotation from the speed sensor accurately and to operate the system normally.
