# Body Interior and Exterior (Part 2)

## EXTERIOR (continued)

### Sun-Roof (continued)

#### DISASSEMBLY

<!-- Figure: Sun-roof motor removal, source: BD.pdf page 21 -->

1. Remove the motor.

2. Remove the front drip.

3. Remove the sun shade.

<!-- Figure: Sun shade removal, source: BD.pdf page 21 -->

4. Remove the drive unit and the guide assembly.

<!-- Figure: Drive unit and guide assembly removal, source: BD.pdf page 21 -->

5. Remove the deflector.

<!-- Figure: Deflector frame, source: BD.pdf page 21 -->

6. Remove the spring.

<!-- Figure: Spring removal, source: BD.pdf page 21 -->

#### REASSEMBLY

1. Install the spring base.

<!-- Figure: Spring base installation, source: BD.pdf page 22 -->

2. Install the deflector assembly (After installing the clip, install the link base stopper.)

<!-- Figure: Deflector assembly installation, source: BD.pdf page 22 -->

3. Insert the shutting into the rail.

> **CAUTION**
> Do not confuse the left and right sides.

<!-- Figure: Shutting insertion into rail, source: BD.pdf page 22 -->

4. Install the cable to the guide assembly.

5. Move the lining front rib to be aligned with the slide of the guide assembly.

<!-- Figure: Guide assembly alignment, source: BD.pdf page 22 -->

6. Align the protrusion of the slide with the groove of the shutting.

<!-- Figure: Slide and shutting alignment, source: BD.pdf page 22 -->

7. Grab the pin by finger to avoid interference with other parts.

<!-- Figure: Pin positioning, source: BD.pdf page 22 -->

8. Place the protrusion of "shoe A" upward, the cushion of "shoe B" downward.

<!-- Figure: Shoe A and B orientation, source: BD.pdf page 23 -->

9. Fully insert the guide assembly into the rail.

<!-- Figure: Guide assembly insertion into rail, source: BD.pdf page 23 -->

10. Completely push the slide until it touches the timing front using a screw driver.

<!-- Figure: Slide pushed to timing front, source: BD.pdf page 23 -->

11. Insert the motor cable into the drive unit assembly tube.

<!-- Figure: Motor cable insertion, source: BD.pdf page 23 -->

12. Install the drive unit assembly to the rail.

<!-- Figure: Drive unit assembly installation, source: BD.pdf page 23 -->

13. Install the sun shade.

<!-- Figure: Sun shade installation, source: BD.pdf page 23 -->

14. Install the sun shade cam to the groove of the sun-roof rail.

<!-- Figure: Sun shade cam installation, source: BD.pdf page 23 -->

15. Install the front drip.

16. Install the motor and controller, and connect the connector.

<!-- Figure: Motor and controller installation, source: BD.pdf page 23 -->

#### INSTALLATION

Installation is the reverse of removal.

#### ADJUSTMENT

1. With the ignition key off and the sun-roof closed down, press the close/down switches simultaneously, during pressing the switches, turn the ignition key "ON", and then the motor adjusts the position automatically.

<!-- Figure: Sun-roof switch panel for adjustment, source: BD.pdf page 24 -->

2. Use the hexagonal wrench for manual adjustment.

<!-- Figure: Manual adjustment with hexagonal wrench, source: BD.pdf page 24 -->

### Windshield Glass

#### COMPONENTS

<!-- Figure: Windshield glass component diagram showing molding positions at sections A-A, B-B, C-C, D-D with glass, molding, roof panel, hood panel, sealant, and inside rear view mirror, source: BD.pdf page 37 -->

#### REMOVAL

1. To remove the windshield, first remove the following parts:
   1) Front pillar trims
   2) Inside rear view mirror
   3) Wiper arms
   4) Weatherstrip and cowl top cover
   5) Windshield glass molding

2. Use the special tool (09861-31100) to cut through the sealant.

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09861-31100 cutting sealant, source: BD.pdf page 38 -->

3. Make mating marks on the glass and body if the glass is to be reinstalled.

4. Take out the windshield glass with the special tool (09861-31400).

<!-- Figure: Windshield glass removal with special tool, source: BD.pdf page 38 -->

#### INSTALLATION

1. Using a cutter knife or the special tool (09861-31200), scrape the old sealant smoothly to a thickness of about 2 mm (0.08 in.) on the bonding surface around the entire windshield flange.

> **CAUTION**
> Be careful not to remove more adhesive than necessary, and also not to damage the paintwork on the body surface with the knife. If the paintwork is damaged, repair the damaged area with touch-up paint.

<!-- Figure: Old sealant scraping, source: BD.pdf page 38 -->

2. Clean the body bonding surface with a sponge dampened in alcohol or wax and grease remover.

3. Install the spacer in the windshield aperture.

<!-- Figure: Spacer installation, source: BD.pdf page 38 -->

4. Center a new windshield glass in the opening. Mark the location by marking line across the glass and body with a grease pencil at the four points as shown.

<!-- Figure: Windshield positioning marks, source: BD.pdf page 39 -->

5. Install the windshield glass molding without any gaps.

6. Glue the rubber dam to the inside surface of the windshield glass around the entire edge as shown, to contain the sealant during installation.

| Part | "A" | Remarks |
|---|---|---|
| Upper | 13.5 mm | Glass molding |
| Side | 13.5 mm | sub |

<!-- Figure: Rubber dam installation with dimensions, source: BD.pdf page 39 -->

7. Apply a light coat of glass primer to the outside of the dam.

> **NOTE**
> 1. Never touch the surface applied primer with your hand.

If you do, the adhesive may not bond to the glass properly, causing a leak after the windshield glass is installed.

2. Do not apply body primer to the glass.

3. Keep water, dust, and abrasive materials away from the surface applied primer.

<!-- Figure: Primer application, source: BD.pdf page 39 -->

8. Apply a primer to the lower glass as shown.

<!-- Figure: Lower glass primer application with 20mm dimension markings, source: BD.pdf page 39 -->

9. Apply a sealant around the edge of the glass.

<!-- Figure: Sealant application cross-section diagrams showing dimensions for upper/side (13.5mm sealant, 12mm glass, molding) and lower (12.5mm), source: BD.pdf page 39 -->

> **NOTE**
> Apply the adhesive within 5 minutes after applying the primer to the glass.

10. Apply a light coat of body primer to the original sealant remaining around the window opening flange. The glass should be installed within 5 minutes after you apply the primer.

<!-- Figure: Body primer application to flange, source: BD.pdf page 40 -->

11. Using suction cups or the special tool (09861-31400), lower the glass over the opening, align it with the marks made in step 4 and set it down on the sealant.

<!-- Figure: Glass installation with special tool 09861-31400, source: BD.pdf page 40 -->

12. Scrape or wipe excess adhesive off with a putty knife or gauze. Fill all cavities around the windshield glass.

13. Perform a water-leak test for the windshield. Use a cold water spray, being careful not to direct a powerful stream of water on the new adhesive material. Allow water to spill over the edges of the glass.

14. If there are leaks, dry the affected area, then apply sealant.

15. Install the removed parts.

> **CAUTION**
> 1. Keep the windshield dry for the first hour after installation.
> 2. Let the car stand for at least four hours after windshield installation. If the car has to be used within the first 8 hours, it must be driven slowly.
> 3. Take care not to slam the doors with all the windows rolled up.
> 4. Take care not to twist the vehicle excessively (such as when going in and out of driveways at an angle or driving over rough, uneven roads).
