## TROUBLESHOOTING

<!-- Reference: ESOB0176 -->

| Trouble symptom | Probable cause | Remedy |
|-----------------|---------------|--------|
| **Water leaks from sunroof** | Dirt accumulated in drain tube | Clear dirt inside of drain |
| | Clogged drain tube | Blow air into drain to remove dirt |
| | Broken or distorted drain tube, defective or cracked clip | Check tube installation and flange contact |
| | Deteriorated or cut weatherstrip | Replace |
| | Excessive roof lid-to-body clearance and improperly fitted weatherstrip | Adjust |
| **Wind noise around sunroof** | Loose or deformed deflector, gaps in body work | Realign adjuster or replace deflector |
| **Sunroof makes a noise when moved** | Foreign particles lodged in guide rail | Check drive cable and guide rails for foreign particles. Relubricate. |
| | Loose guide rails and lid | |
| **Motor runs but sunroof does not move or moves only halfway** | Foreign particles lodged in guide rail | Check drive cable and guide rails |
| | Incorrect engagement of motor pinion with drive cable | Check for loose motor installation and damaged pinion |
| | Decrease in motor's clutch slipping force | Adjust |
| | Increased sunroof sliding resistance or interference of sunroof with drive cables, weatherstrip, etc. due to maladjustment of sunroof | Adjust or replace |
| **Noise in motor (clutch slipping noise from motor when sunroof is fully opened or closed but is not an unusual noise)** | Incorrect engagement of motor pinion with drive cable | Check pinion installation and re-tighten motor |
| | Worn out or damaged motor pinion bearing | Replace motor assembly |
| | Worn out or defective drive cable | Replace |
| **Door glass fails to operate up and down** | Incorrect window glass installation | Adjust position |
| | Damaged or faulty regulator arm or regulator | Correct or replace |
| **Door does not open or close completely** | Incorrect door installation | Adjust position |
| | Defective door check assembly | Correct or replace |
| | Door hinge requires grease | Apply grease |
| **Hood does not open or close completely** | Striker and latch not properly aligned | Adjust |
| | Incorrectly installed hood | Adjust |
| | Incorrect hood bumper height | Adjust |
| **Water leak through windshield and rear window** | Defective seal | Fill with sealant |
| | Defective flange | Correct |
| **Wind noise around door** | Weatherstrip not holding firmly | Adjust fit of door |
| | Improperly installed weatherstrip | Repair or replace |
| | Improperly closed door | Adjust |
| | Improperly fit door | Adjust |
| | Improper clearance between door glass and division channel | Adjust |
| | Deformed door | Repair or replace |

# EXTERIOR

## FENDER

### COMPONENTS

<!-- Figure: Fender component overview showing front fender assembly on vehicle, source: BD.pdf page 8 -->

### REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION

<!-- Reference: ESOB0400 -->

> **NOTE:** In order to prevent the fender mounting bolt area from forming rust, apply tape sealer between the fender and the body panel.

1. Remove the front mud guard and wheel guard.

<!-- Figure: Mud guard and wheel guard removal, source: BD.pdf page 9 -->

2. Remove the 3 bolts holding the head lamp and disconnect the wire connector. Remove the head lamp assembly.

<!-- Figure: Head lamp bolt removal, source: BD.pdf page 9 -->

3. Remove the front bumper. (refer to front bumper group)

4. Remove the fender mounting bolts and remove the fender panel assembly.

> **CAUTION:** Use care to avoid damaging the paint.

<!-- Figure: Fender mounting bolt locations, source: BD.pdf page 9 -->

5. Installation is the reverse of the removal.

## FENDER GARNISH

<!-- Reference: ESOB0500 -->

### REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION

1. Remove the wheel guard.

<!-- Figure: Wheel guard removal for fender garnish access, source: BD.pdf page 10 -->

2. Loosen the fender garnish mounting screws and then remove the fender garnish.

<!-- Figure: Fender garnish removal showing screw, garnish, and fender panel locations, source: BD.pdf page 10 -->
<!-- Figure: Fender garnish clip detail, source: BD.pdf page 10 -->

3. Installation is the reverse of the removal.

> **CAUTION:**
> 1. When installing garnish, be careful not to deform the fender panel.
> 2. Engage the clip part on the garnish into the fender panel securely until it makes "click" sound.

<!-- Reference: ESOB0700 -->

#### HOOD HINGE

1. Loosen the hood hinge mounting bolt.

1. Loosen the hood lock mounting bolts.

## FRONT DOOR

### REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION

<!-- Reference: ESOB1100 -->

1. Remove the grip handle using a remover.

<!-- Figure: Grip handle removal, source: BD.pdf page 16 -->

2. Remove the inside handle housing.

<!-- Figure: Inside handle housing removal, source: BD.pdf page 16 -->

3. Remove the tweeter speaker using a remover.

<!-- Figure: Tweeter speaker removal, source: BD.pdf page 16 -->

4. Remove the mounting screws and remove the door trim panel. Disconnect the wire connectors.

<!-- Figure: Door trim panel removal, source: BD.pdf page 16 -->

5. Remove the door grip handle mounting bracket, speaker and door trim seal.

<!-- Figure: Door grip handle bracket, speaker and trim seal removal, source: BD.pdf page 16 -->

6. Remove the door tooling hole cover.

<!-- Figure: Door tooling hole cover removal, source: BD.pdf page 16 -->

7. Remove the door glass stopper catch.

<!-- Figure: Door glass stopper catch removal, source: BD.pdf page 16 -->

**Tightening torque:**
4-6 Nm (40-60 kg-cm, 3-5 lb-ft)

8. Remove the door delta chassis.

**Tightening torque:**
9-14 Nm (90-140 kg-cm, 6.5-10 lb-ft)

<!-- Figure: Door delta chassis removal, source: BD.pdf page 17 -->

9. Remove the glass stopper bolts, remove the window glass.

<!-- Figure: Window glass removal, source: BD.pdf page 17 -->

10. Remove the door side weather strip.

<!-- Figure: Door side weather strip removal, source: BD.pdf page 17 -->

11. Loosen the mounting nuts and remove the regulator assembly.

<!-- Figure: Regulator assembly removal, source: BD.pdf page 17 -->

12. Disconnect the cable, after loosening the inner handle mounting screw, push the inner handle toward the direction shown in the illustration and remove it.

<!-- Figure: Inner handle cable disconnection and removal, source: BD.pdf page 17 -->

13. Remove the door outside belt.

<!-- Figure: Door outside belt removal, source: BD.pdf page 17 -->

> **NOTE:** Install the door outside belt to the inside of groove of the door panel.

<!-- Figure: Door outside belt installation note, source: BD.pdf page 17 -->

14. Remove the door outside handle and key cylinder.

<!-- Figure: Door outside handle and key cylinder removal, source: BD.pdf page 18 -->

15. Remove the door latch assembly mounting screws and remove the door latch assembly.

<!-- Figure: Door latch assembly removal, source: BD.pdf page 18 -->

**Tightening torque:**
17-22 Nm (170-220 kg-cm, 12-16 lb-ft)

16. Remove the door checker.

<!-- Figure: Door checker removal, source: BD.pdf page 18 -->

17. Installation is the reverse of removal.

> When installing the door vapor barrier, butyl tape should not be placed so it blocks the door trim fastener holes.

### DOOR POSITION ADJUSTMENT

<!-- Reference: ESOB1200 -->

Check for a flush fit with the body, then check for an equal gap between the front and rear, top and bottom door edges and body.

The door and body edges should also be parallel.

> **CAUTION:** Attach protective tape to the fender edges where the hinge is installed.

### DOOR STRIKER ADJUSTMENT

<!-- Reference: ESOB1300 -->

Make sure the door is not loose, and that it latches securely without slamming. If necessary, adjust the door as follows:

1. Draw a line around the striker plate for reference.

2. Loosen the striker screws and move the striker IN or OUT to make the latch fit tighter or looser. Move the striker UP or DOWN to align it with the latch opening. Then tightly tighten the screws and recheck.

> **NOTE:** Hold the outside handle out and push the door against the body to ensure the striker allows a flush fit.

<!-- Figure: Door striker adjustment illustration, source: BD.pdf page 18 -->
