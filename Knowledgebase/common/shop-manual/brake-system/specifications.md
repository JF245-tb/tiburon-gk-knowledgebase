## Service Brake Pedal Inspection and Adjustment EJOC0061

1. Turn the stop lamp switch until the clearance between the stop lamp switch outer case and pedal arm reaches the standard value, and secure the stop lamp switch with the lock nut.

   **Clearance between pedal and stop lamp switch A:**
   0.5–1.0 mm (0.02–0.039 in.)

<!-- figure: EJOA0038 — Stop lamp switch clearance illustration -->

2. Free play of brake pedal.

   **Brake pedal free play B:**
   3–8 mm (0.117–0.312 in.)

<!-- figure: KGOH5R1A — Brake pedal free play illustration -->

## Brake Booster Operation Test Without a Tester EJOC0070

For a simple check of brake booster operation, perform the following tests.

1. Run the engine for one or two minutes, and then shut it off. Depress the brake pedal several times at normal foot pressure. If the pedal goes down further at first but rises gradually after the second or third time, the brake booster is functioning properly. If the pedal height doesn't change, the booster is damaged.

2. With the engine off, depress the brake pedal several times and check the pedal height changes. After that, with the pedal depressed, start the engine. If the pedal goes down slightly, the booster is in good condition. If the pedal height doesn't change, the booster is damaged.

<!-- BR-7 -->
3. With the engine running, depress the brake pedal and then stop the engine. Hold the pedal depressed for 30 seconds. If the pedal height does not change, the booster is in good condition. If the pedal goes up, the booster is damaged.

   If one of the above three tests is not okay, check the vacuum hoses and the brake booster and make any necessary corrections. If all tests are OK, the booster is good.

## Check Valve Operation Check

When checking the check valve, keep the check valve fit in the vacuum hose.

1. Remove the vacuum hose.

> **NOTE**
> The check valve is press-fitted inside the vacuum hose at the position of the marking.

<!-- figure: EJOBR1A4 — Vacuum hose, master cylinder, check valve illustration -->

2. Check the operation of the check valve by using a vacuum pump.

| Vacuum pump connection | Accept/Reject criteria |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| Connection at the brake booster side (A) | A negative pressure (vacuum) is created and held |
| Connection at the intake manifold side (B) | A negative pressure (vacuum) is not created |

> **CAUTION**
> *If the check valve is defective, replace it if an assembly unit together with the vacuum hose.*

<!-- figure: EJOA0R2A — Check valve cross-section (Valve, Spring, Brake booster side, Intake manifold side) -->

## Bleeding the Brake System EJOC0080

1. Remove the reservoir cap and fill the brake reservoir with brake fluid.

> **CAUTION**
> *If there is any brake fluid on any painted surface, wash it off immediately.*

> **NOTE**
> When bleeding pressurized fluid, do not depress the brake pedal.

<!-- figure: KGOA2RO1A — Brake reservoir illustration -->

<!-- BR-8 -->
2. Connect a vinyl tube to the wheel cylinder bleeder plug, and insert the other end of tube in the container of brake fluid.

3. Depress the brake pedal several times.

4. While depressing the brake pedal fully, loosen the bleeder plug until the fluid starts to run out. Then close the bleeder plug.

5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 until there are no more bubbles in the fluid.

6. Tighten the bleeder plug screw.

   **Bleeder screw tightening torque:**
   7–13 Nm (70–130 kgf.cm, 5.2–9.6 lb.ft)

7. Repeat the above procedure for each wheel in the sequence shown in the illustration.

<!-- figure: EJCA0470 — Brake bleeding sequence illustration (numbered 1-4, starting from right rear) -->

## Adjustment of Parking Brake Stroke EJOC0060

1. Pull the brake lever with a force of approx. 196 N (20 kg, 44 lbs.) and check the number of clicks.

   **Parking brake lever stroke: 6–7 clicks**

2. If the number of notches is not the standard value, adjust the cable length with the adjusting nut on the equalizer.

<!-- figure: KGOKSR01A — Parking brake lever illustration -->
<!-- figure: EGOAR02A — Equalizer, parking brake lever, adjusting nut illustration -->

3. The indicator light will go out when the brake lever is fully released, and will light up with the lever pulled one notch. If it does not operate properly, replace it.

4. After the adjustment, check that the rear brakes do not drag with the parking brake lever released.
