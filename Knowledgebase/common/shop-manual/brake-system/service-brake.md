# Service Brake System

## Brake Pedal

<!-- EJKB0090 -->

### Components

<!-- Figure: Brake pedal exploded view — stop lamp switch, bracket member assembly, bolt, pedal bushing (x2), return spring, brake pedal, source: BR.pdf page 10 -->

| Fastener | Torque |
|----------|--------|
| Pedal bushing bolt | 8-12 Nm (80-120 kgf.cm, 5.9-8.9 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE: Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft)**

### Removal
<!-- EJKB0100 -->

1. Remove the stop lamp switch.
2. Remove the split pin and clevis pin.
3. Remove the flange nuts of the brake mounting bracket.
4. Remove the brake pedal assembly.

<!-- Figure: Brake pedal removal, source: BR.pdf page 11 -->

### Installation
<!-- EJKB0110 -->

1. Installation is the reverse of removal.

> **NOTE**
> Be sure to install the split pin on the operating rod clevis pin.

2. Install the brake pedal assembly and tighten the flange nuts (booster mounting nuts) and bolt.

**Tightening torque:**

| Fastener | Torque |
|----------|--------|
| Flange nuts / bolt | 13-16 Nm (130-160 kgf.cm, 9.6 lb.ft) |

3. Adjust the brake pedal height and free play.
4. Install the stop lamp switch.

<!-- Figure: Brake pedal installation, source: BR.pdf page 11 -->

### Inspection
<!-- EJKB0131 -->

1. Check the bushing for wear.
2. Check the brake pedal for distortion.
3. Check the brake pedal return spring for damage.
4. Check the stop lamp switch:
   1. With an ohmmeter connected to the stop lamp switch terminals, check for continuity.
   2. If there is no continuity when the plunger is depressed and there is continuity when the plunger is released, the stop lamp switch is normal.

<!-- Figure: Brake switch check, source: BR.pdf page 11 -->

## Brake Booster

### Component
<!-- EJKB0140 -->

<!-- Figure: Brake booster exploded view — clamp, spacer, clevis pin, split pin, master cylinder, vacuum hose, brake booster assembly, source: BR.pdf page 12 -->

| Fastener | Torque |
|----------|--------|
| Master cylinder to booster | 13-16 Nm (130-160 kgf.cm, 9.6-11.8 lb.ft) |
| Booster mounting nuts | 13-16 Nm (130-160 kgf.cm, 9.6-11.8 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE: Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft)**

### Removal
<!-- EJKB0146 -->

> **CAUTION**
> If there is any brake fluid on any painted surface, wash it off immediately.

1. Remove the air cleaner.

<!-- Figure: Master cylinder and brake booster on vehicle, source: BR.pdf page 12 -->

2. Disconnect the brake tube from the master cylinder.
3. Remove the master cylinder.
4. Disconnect the vacuum hose from the booster.
5. Remove the operating rod from the brake pedal.
6. Loosen the booster mounting nuts.
7. Lift out the booster assembly.

<!-- Figure: Booster assembly removal, source: BR.pdf page 13 -->

### Installation
<!-- EJKB0150 -->

1. When the booster assembly is installed, replace the packing at each end of the booster mounting holder, if necessary.
2. Install the brake booster and tighten the mounting nuts.

**Tightening torque:**

| Fastener | Torque |
|----------|--------|
| Booster mounting nuts | 13-16 Nm (130-160 kgf.cm, 9.6-11.8 lb.ft) |

3. Apply sufficient grease inside the brake pedal hole.
4. Connect the clevis to the brake pedal with the clevis pin and install the split pin to the clevis pin.

<!-- Figure: Brake pedal, clevis pin, and split pin connection, source: BR.pdf page 13 -->

5. Install the master cylinder and connect the brake tube to the master cylinder.
6. Connect the vacuum hose to the brake booster.
7. Fill the brake reservoir with brake fluid and bleed the system.
8. Check for fluid leakage.
9. Check and adjust the brake pedal.
10. After installation, apply sufficient grease to contact points of the clevis and brake pedal.

## Master Cylinder

### Component
<!-- EJCI0006 -->

<!-- Figure: Master cylinder exploded view — reservoir cap, brake fluid filter, reservoir assembly, grommet, cylinder pin, primary piston assembly, secondary piston assembly, master cylinder body, source: BR.pdf page 14 -->

### Removal
<!-- EJCI0008 -->

1. Remove the fluid level warning device connector.
2. Detach the brake tubes from the master cylinder, and then install the plug.

> **CAUTION**
> If there is any brake fluid on any painted surface, wash it off immediately.

3. Remove master cylinder mounting nuts and then lift out the master cylinder.

<!-- Figure: Vacuum hose, master cylinder on vehicle, source: BR.pdf page 14 -->

### Disassembly
<!-- EJCI0016 -->

1. Remove the reservoir cap and drain the brake fluid into a suitable container.
2. After disconnecting the mounting screws, pry the reservoir free from the master cylinder.

<!-- Figure: Cylinder pin location, source: BR.pdf page 15 -->

3. Using a snap ring pliers, remove the retainer ring.
4. Remove the primary cylinder pin, pushing the primary piston with a screwdriver and remove the primary piston assembly.

<!-- Figure: Cylinder pin removal, source: BR.pdf page 15 -->

5. Remove the secondary cylinder pin. Push the secondary piston with a screwdriver and remove the secondary piston assembly.

> **NOTE**
> Do not disassemble the primary and secondary piston assembly.

### Inspection
<!-- EJKB0160 -->

1. Check the master cylinder bore for rust or scoring.
2. Check the master cylinder for wear or damage. If necessary, clean or replace the cylinder.

> **NOTE**
> 1. If the cylinder bore is damaged, replace the master cylinder assembly.
> 2. Wash the parts in alcohol.

### Reassembly
<!-- EJCI0060 -->

1. Apply genuine brake fluid to the rubber parts of the cylinder kit and grommets.

<!-- Figure: Reassembly illustration, source: BR.pdf page 15 -->

2. Carefully insert the springs and pistons in the proper direction.
3. Press against the pistons with a screwdriver and install the retainer ring.
4. With the piston pushed completely by a screwdriver, install the cylinder pin.

<!-- Figure: Cylinder pin installation, source: BR.pdf page 16 -->

5. Mount two grommets.
6. Install the reservoir on the cylinder.

### Installation
<!-- EJKB0180 -->

1. Install the master cylinder to the brake booster.
2. Install the booster and master cylinder to the dash panel.
3. Connect the brake tubes to the master cylinder.
4. Install the vacuum hose tightly.
5. Connect the operating rod to the brake pedal.
6. After filling the brake reservoir with the brake fluid, bleed the system.

> **CAUTION**
> Don't disassemble the brake booster.

## Front Disc Brake

### Components
<!-- EJKB0810 -->

<!-- Figure: Front disc brake exploded view — connecting tube (brake hose and brake tube), front brake assembly, brake disc, source: BR.pdf page 17 -->

**Removal order:**
1. Connecting tube (brake hose and brake tube)
2. Front brake assembly
3. Brake disc

| Fastener | Torque |
|----------|--------|
| Front caliper guide rod bolt | 65-75 Nm (650-750 kgf.cm, 48-55 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE: Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft)**

### Brake Pad

#### Inspection
<!-- EJKB0820 -->

1. Check the pads for wear or oil contamination and replace, if necessary.

> **CAUTION**
> - The pads for the right and left wheels should be replaced at the same time. Never "drop" or intermix brake pad sets.
> - All four pads must be replaced as a complete set.
> - When replacing the brake pad, check for deformation. When replacing the guide spring, clean or use a new one after clearing away every foreign material.

2. Check the pad for damage or deformation.

**Brake pad thickness:**

| Measurement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Standard value | 11 mm (0.43 in.) |
| Service limited value | 2 mm (0.079 in.) |

<!-- Figure: Brake pad thickness measurement — shim, inner pad, outer pad, source: BR.pdf page 17-18 -->

#### Removal
<!-- EJKB0830 -->

1. Remove the 2 guide pin bolt, remove the caliper assembly from the knuckle and secure it with a wire or some other retaining method.
2. Remove the pads.

> **CAUTION**
> Do not depress the brake pedal while disassembling the pads.

#### Installation
<!-- EJCI0096 -->

1. Install the pad clips.
2. Install the pads onto each pad clip.

> **NOTE**
> Position the pad with its pad wear indicator upward on the piston side.

<!-- Figure: Guide spring positioning, source: BR.pdf page 18 -->

3. Insert the piston in the cylinder using the Special Tool (09581-11000).
4. Install the new pads. The shims are attached to the each pad as illustrated.

> **CAUTION**
> Be careful so that the disc or pad isn't contaminated by grease.

5. Install the bolt and tighten to the specified value.

**Tightening torque:**

| Fastener | Torque |
|----------|--------|
| Guide rod bolt | 22-32 Nm (220-320 kgf.cm, 16-24 lb.ft) |

### Caliper Assembly

#### Removal
<!-- EJKB0870 -->

1. Remove the wheel and tire.
2. Disconnect the brake hose.
3. Remove the cylinder mounting bolt.
4. Remove cylinder and pads.
5. Remove the caliper mounting bolts (2EA) from the knuckle.
6. Remove the caliper.

<!-- Figure: Caliper removal steps, source: BR.pdf page 18 -->

#### Disassembly
<!-- EJKB0880 -->

1. Remove the piston boot.
2. Remove the piston using compressed air.

> **NOTE**
> 1. Do not put your fingers in front of the piston when using compressed air.
> 2. Be careful not to splatter the brake fluid.

3. Remove the piston seal from the caliper by using a screwdriver.

<!-- Figure: Piston removal with compressed air, source: BR.pdf page 19 -->

#### Inspection
<!-- EJKB0870F -->

1. Check the caliper for wear, damage, cracks and dust.
2. Check the piston for dust, damage, cracks and wear on the outer surface.
3. Check the sleeve and pin for damage and dust.
4. Check the pad spring and boots for damage.
5. Check the carrier for damage, dust, wear and cracks.

> **CAUTION**
> 1. Do not use sand paper on the piston surface.
> 2. All rubber parts must be replaced with new parts.

6. Inspect the disc by using a calipers and a dial gauge.

**Thickness of disc mm (in.):**

| Measurement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Standard value | 25 (1.024) |
| Service limit | 24.4 (0.961) |
| Runout of the disc mm (in.) | 0.06 (0.003) |
| Difference of thickness | 0.01 mm (0.0004 in.) |

> **NOTE**
> 1. Using a micrometer, measure the disc thickness at eight positions approximately 15 mm from the outer edge of the disc and at 45° intervals. If you subtract the minimum of measurements from the maximum, you get the difference of the disc thickness.
> 2. When measuring the disc runout, fix a dial gauge approximately 5 mm from the outer edge of the disc. At this time, if you subtract the minimum of measurements from the maximum, you get the disc runout.

7. If necessary, replace the brake disc.

#### Reassembly
<!-- EJCI0098 -->

1. Clean all components except the pads and shims with isopropyl alcohol.
2. Apply rubber grease on the piston seal and install the piston seal in the cylinder.

<!-- Figure: Piston and piston boot installation, source: BR.pdf page 19 -->

3. Assemble the piston and piston boots according to the following procedure:
   1. Apply rubber grease to the caliper bore, the outside surface of the piston and the piston boot.
   2. Install the piston boot on the piston as illustrated.
   3. Insert the piston boot in the inner groove of the caliper and push the piston into the caliper.

<!-- Figure: Piston boot assembly — hammer, narrow chisel, piston boot, source: BR.pdf page 20 -->

4. Assemble the sliding parts according to the following procedure:
   1. Apply rubber grease to the outside surface of the sleeve and pin, on and bore of the caliper, pin boot and sleeve boot.
   2. Insert the boot into the groove of the caliper.

<!-- Figure: Pin side assembly — pin boot, guide pin, sleeve boot, source: BR.pdf page 20 -->

5. Install the pads.

> **NOTE**
> Be careful so that the disc or pad is contaminated by grease.

6. Tighten the brake hose connecting bolt.

**Tightening torque:**

| Fastener | Torque |
|----------|--------|
| Sliding pin | 34-44 Nm (350-450 kgf.cm, 26-33 lb.ft) |
| Carrier mounting bolt | 64-74 Nm (650-750 kgf.cm, 48-54 lb.ft) |
| Brake hose mounting set bolt | 25-29 Nm (250-300 kgf.cm, 18-22 lb.ft) |

> **NOTE**
> 1. Check that the surface of the pin and bolts is not damaged before tightening.
> 2. Bleed the system. Depress the pedal several times and check for fluid leakage from all connecting parts.

## Rear Disc Brake

### Component
<!-- EJCI0099 -->

<!-- Figure: Rear disc brake exploded view — spring washer, return spring, lever, cam boot, cam, piston boot, piston, piston seal, caliper assembly, source: BR.pdf page 21 -->

| Fastener | Torque |
|----------|--------|
| Lever bolt | 22-32 Nm (220-320 kgf.cm, 16-24 lb.ft) |
| Cam bolt | 7-9 Nm (70-90 kgf.cm, 5.2-6.7 lb.ft) |
| Caliper assembly | 200-260 Nm (2000-2600 kgf.cm, 148-192 lb.ft) |
| Return spring bolt | 25-35 Nm (250-350 kgf.cm, 18-22 lb.ft) |

**TORQUE: Nm (kgf.cm, lb.ft)**

### Removal
<!-- EJCI0100 -->

1. Remove the wheel.
2. Remove the brake fluid hose.

<!-- Figure: Brake hose, parking cable, caliper assembly removal, source: BR.pdf page 22 -->

3. Remove the caliper assembly mounting bolts (2EA).
4. Remove the brake pad and caliper assembly.
5. Remove the parking brake cable after disconnecting the parking brake cable mounting clip.

### Inspection
<!-- EJCI0097 -->

1. Check the pads for wear or oil contamination and replace if necessary.

> **NOTE**
> The pads for the right and left wheels should be replaced at the same time.

**Brake pad thickness:**

| Measurement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Standard value | 9 mm (0.354 in.) |
| Service limited value | 2 mm (0.079 in.) |

<!-- Figure: Pad wear comparison — new vs worn, pad wear indicator, brake disc, source: BR.pdf page 22 -->

2. Check the leading and trailing shoe keys and retaining screw for damage, or wear. Replace the keys and retaining screw at the same time when the pads are replaced.
3. Check dust boots for wear or damage. If dust or mud has entered the caliper assembly through this seal, the caliper assembly must be replaced.
4. Before replacing the brake pads, drain the brake fluid from the master cylinder reservoir until it is half full.
5. Inspect the disc by using a calipers and a dial gauge.

**Thickness of disc mm (in.):**

| Measurement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Standard value | 10 (0.394) |
| Service limit | 8 (0.315) |
| Runout of the disc | 0.08 (0.003) |
| Difference of thickness | 0.01 (0.0004) |

> **NOTE**
> 1. Using a micrometer, measure the disc thickness at eight positions approximately 5 mm from the outer edge of the disc and at 45° intervals. If you subtract the minimum of measurements from the maximum, you get the difference of the disc thickness.
> 2. When measuring the disc runout, fix a dial gauge approximately 5 mm from the outer edge of the disc, and rotate the disc 360°. At this time, if you subtract the minimum of measurements from the maximum, you get the disc runout.

6. If necessary, replace the brake disc.

### Installation
<!-- EJCI0098 -->

1. Check the piston bore and piston for wear, damage, or rust. Replace damaged parts if necessary.
2. Check the piston seal, boot, and cam boot for wear or tear.
3. Apply recommended lubricant to the following parts.

| Parts | Recommended Lubricant | Quantity |
|-------|----------------------|----------|
| Piston seal | Brake fluid (Dot 3 or Dot 4) | As required |
| Cylinder inner side | Brake fluid (Dot 3 or Dot 4) | As required |
| Piston boot | Brake fluid (Dot 3 or Dot 4) | As required |
| Piston outer surface | Brake fluid (Dot 3 or Dot 4) | As required |
| Guide rod insulators | Rubber grease | As required |

4. Push the piston into the caliper with the special tool.

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09580-34000 for piston installation, source: BR.pdf page 23 -->

## Proportioning Valve
<!-- EJCI0094 -->

The proportioning valve distributes the proper fluid pressure to the front and rear wheels to obtain greater braking efficiency and prevents premature rear wheel lock-up. You should not disassemble it because the performance of the valve is closely connected with the mounting tension of the spring.

### Proportioning Valve Function Test

1. Remove the proportioning valve from the master cylinder.
2. Connect two pressure gauges; one to the input side, and one to the output side.

> **NOTE**
> Be sure to bleed the system after connecting the pressure gauges.

3. With the brakes applied, measure the input pressure and the output pressure. If the measured pressures are within the specified range as illustrated, the proportioning valve is good.
4. Reconnect the brake lines in their original positions and bleed the system.

> **NOTE**
> This figure shows characteristics of the proportioning valve as the pressure increases.

### Installation of Proportioning Valve
<!-- EJCI0095 -->

1. Install the master cylinder according to the illustration.

<!-- Figure: Master cylinder and proportioning valve assembly, source: BR.pdf page 24 -->

2. Tighten the flare nuts and bleed the system.

**Tightening torque:**

| Brake tube flare nut | 13-17 Nm (130-170 kgf.cm, 9-12 lb.ft) |

## Brake Line

### Components
<!-- EJKB0210 -->

#### CBS (Conventional Brake System)

<!-- Figure: CBS brake line layout — master cylinder, brake booster, FR, RR, FL, RL, source: BR.pdf page 25 -->

#### ABS (Anti-lock Brake System)

<!-- Figure: ABS brake line layout — brake booster, master cylinder, hydraulic unit, front wheel speed sensors (FR, FL), rear wheel speed sensors (RR, RL), source: BR.pdf page 25 -->

### Inspection
<!-- EJDA0630 -->

1. Check the brake tubes for cracks, crimps and corrosion.
2. Check the brake hoses for cracks, damage and leakage.
3. Check the brake tube flare nuts for damage and leakage.

### Installation
<!-- EJKB0220 -->

1. Install the brake hoses so as they do not twist.
2. The brake tubes should be installed away from edges, weld beads or moving parts.
3. Tighten the connections to the specified torque.

**Tightening torque:**

| Fastener | Torque |
|----------|--------|
| Flare nuts | 13-17 Nm (130-170 kgf.cm, 9.6-12 lb.ft) |
| Brake hose to front caliper | 25-30 Nm (250-300 kgf.cm, 18-22 lb.ft) |

## Parking Brake System

### Parking Brake
<!-- EJCI0410 -->

<!-- Figure: Parking brake assembly — adjusting nut, parking brake cable, parking brake lever, source: BR.pdf page 27 -->

### Removal
<!-- EJCI0500 -->

1. Remove the rear console.
2. Loosen the adjusting nut and detach the parking brake cable.
3. Detach the parking brake switch assembly.
4. Remove the parking brake lever assembly.

<!-- Figure: Parking brake lever removal, source: BR.pdf page 27 -->

5. Remove the wheel.
6. Loosen the parking brake cable clamp and remove the parking brake cable assembly.

<!-- Figure: Parking brake cable routing — lever, parking brake cable, source: BR.pdf page 28 -->

7. Detach the parking brake mounting clip, and then remove the parking brake cable from the lever.
8. Remove the caliper mounting bolts (2).
9. Remove the brake pads and caliper.

### Inspection
<!-- EJCI0496 -->

1. Check the parking brake switch operation.
2. Check the parking brake lever ratchet for wear.
3. Check the parking brake cable for wear or damage.
4. Check the pads for wear or oil contamination and replace, if necessary.

### Installation
<!-- EJCI0494 -->

1. Check the parking brake cables for left and right identification marks and install accordingly.

**Identification color:**

| Side | Color |
|------|-------|
| Left side | Blue |
| Right side | White |

2. Install the parking brake cable in the direction shown in the illustration.

<!-- Figure: Parking brake cable routing direction, source: BR.pdf page 28 -->

3. Apply the specified grease to the moving parts of the ratchet plate and ratchet pawl.

**Specified grease:**
Multipurpose grease SAE J310, NLGI No.2

4. After installing the cable adjuster, adjust the parking brake lever stroke.

> **NOTE**
> When holding the point where is at a distance of 40 mm from the end of the lever, by hand and pulling up, adjust the nut adjusting the lever assembly so that the lever is fixed to 6-7 notches.
