### Removal <!-- SKTC1700 -->

1. Disconnect the negative (-) terminal from the battery.

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09561-11002 steering wheel puller, source: ST.pdf page 13 -->

2. After removing the bolts in the illustration, remove the driver's airbag module.

> **NOTE**
> For the vehicles not equipped with SRS airbag, remove the horn cover assembly.

<!-- Figure: Airbag module bolt removal, source: ST.pdf page 13 -->

3. Remove the steering wheel lock nut.

4. After aligning the marks on the steering shaft and wheel, remove the steering wheel using the special tool.

> **NOTE**
> Do not hammer on the steering wheel to remove it. Doing so it may damage the steering column.

<!-- Figure: Steering wheel removal with puller tool, source: ST.pdf page 13 -->

5. After removing the 3 bolts in the illustration, remove the steering column shroud.

<!-- Figure: Steering column shroud bolt locations, source: ST.pdf page 13 -->

6. Remove the connectors of the multifunction switch and the multifunction switch assembly.

<!-- Figure: Multifunction switch connector removal, source: ST.pdf page 13 -->

7. Remove the lower crash pad after loosening the mounting bolts.

<!-- Figure: Lower crash pad removal, source: ST.pdf page 13 -->

8. Remove the bolts connecting the steering column shaft, the universal joint and the pinion.

<!-- Figure: Universal joint bolt removal at steering column shaft, source: ST.pdf page 14 -->

9. After removing the mounting bolts and nuts, remove the steering column and shaft assembly.

### Inspection <!-- SPHK1800 -->

1. Check the steering column shaft for damage and deformation.
2. Check connectors for play, damage and smooth operation.
3. Check the ball joint bearing for wear and damage.

### Installation <!-- SPHK1900 -->

Assembly is the reverse of removal.

### Disassembly and Reassembly <!-- STKC2800 -->

**Key Lock Assembly**

1. If it is necessary to remove the key lock assembly, use a punch to make a groove on the head of the special bolt, and then use a screwdriver to remove the key lock assembly mounting bracket.

2. Disassemble the key lock assembly from the steering column and shaft assembly.

<!-- Figure: Key lock assembly removal from steering column, source: ST.pdf page 14 -->

3. Reassembly is the reverse of disassembly.

**Universal Joint Assembly**

1. Remove the bolt connecting the universal joint assembly and the steering column and shaft assembly.

<!-- Figure: Universal joint assembly removal, source: ST.pdf page 14 -->

2. Reassembly is the reverse of disassembly.

### Removal <!-- SPKC2200 -->

1. Detach the power steering pressure hose mounting clamp and the return tube mounting clamp.

2. Drain the power steering fluid.

3. Disconnect the pressure tube and the return tube fittings from the gear box.

4. Disconnect the shaft assembly from the gear box inside the passenger compartment.

<!-- Figure: Shaft disconnection from gear box inside passenger compartment, source: ST.pdf page 16 -->

5. Remove the strap and push out the dust cover and remove the dust cover mounting plate.

6. Raise up the vehicle.

7. Remove the front tire (RH/LH).

8. After removing the split pin, disconnect the tie rod from the knuckle by using the special tool (09568-31000).

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09568-31000 separating tie rod from knuckle, source: ST.pdf page 16 -->

9. Remove the stabilizer bar (RH side) mounting bracket and link nut.

10. After removing the rear roll stopper bracket mounting bolt from the sub frame, remove the rear roll stopper.

<!-- Figure: Rear roll stopper removal, source: ST.pdf page 16 -->

11. After loosing the sub frame mounting bolts a little so as to let the sub frame assembly hung down slightly.

<!-- Figure: Sub frame mounting bolt loosening, source: ST.pdf page 16 -->

12. Remove the power steering gear box mounting bolts, pressure tube and the return tube clamp.

<!-- Figure: Gear box mounting bolt removal, source: ST.pdf page 16 -->

13. Pull the power steering gear box assembly toward the right side of the vehicle.

> **NOTE**
> When removing the gear box, pull it out carefully and slowly to avoid damaging.

<!-- Figure: Power steering gear box removal toward right side of vehicle, source: ST.pdf page 17 -->

### Inspection and Adjustment Before Disassembly <!-- SPKC2400 -->

Fix a brass plate or aluminum plate for protection to the jaws of a vise and mount the gear box in a vise.

> **CAUTION**
> When mounting the gear box in a vise, let the installation section of it be fixed to the jaws. If other section is fixed, the gear box may be damaged.

**Total Pinion Preload**

1. Rotate the pinion gear for approximately 4 to 6 seconds for one rotation to measure the total pinion preload.

**Standard value**

Total pinion preload :
0.6-1.3 Nm (6-13 kg.cm, 0.4-1.0 lb.ft)

> **NOTE**
> Measure the pinion preload through the entire stroke of the rack.

<!-- Figure: Pinion preload measurement, source: ST.pdf page 19 -->

2. If the measured value is out of specifications, first adjust the yoke plug, then recheck the total pinion preload.

3. If not obtain the total pinion preload after adjusting the yoke plug check or replace the yoke plug components.

**Tie Rod Swing Resistance**

1. Rotate the tie rod assembly ten times.

2. Measure the tie rod swing resistance with a spring scale.

**Standard value**

Total rod swing resistance :
2.5 Nm (20-50 kg.cm, 17.4-43 lb.in)

3. If the measured value exceeds the standard value, replace the tie rod assembly.

> **CAUTION**
> Even if the measured value is below the standard value, the tie rod that swings smoothly without excessive play may be used. If the measured value is zero or 0.49 Nm, replace the tie rod.

**Bellows Inspection**

1. Inspect the bellows for damage or deterioration.

2. Make sure the bellows are secured in the correct position.

3. If the bellows are defective, replace them with new ones.

<!-- ST-20 -->
### Disassembly <!-- SPKC2500 -->

1. Remove the tie rod and from the tie rod.

<!-- Figure: Tie rod end removal from tie rod, source: ST.pdf page 20 -->

2. After mounting the tie rod and in a vise, remove the dust cover from the ball joint.

<!-- Figure: Dust cover removal from ball joint in vise, source: ST.pdf page 20 -->

3. Remove the bellows band.

4. Remove the bellows clip.

5. Pull the bellows out toward the tie rod.

> **NOTE**
> Check for rust on the rack when the bellows are replaced.

6. Remove the feed tube from the rack housing.

7. While moving the rack slowly, drain the fluid from the rack housing.

8. Unstake the tab washer which fixes the tie rod and rack with a chisel.

<!-- Figure: Tab washer unstaking with chisel, source: ST.pdf page 20 -->

9. Remove the tie rod from the rack.

> **CAUTION**
> Remove the tie rod from the rack, taking care not to twist the rack.

<!-- ST-21 -->

10. Remove the yoke plug locking nut.

<!-- Figure: Yoke plug locking nut removal, source: ST.pdf page 21 -->

11. Remove the yoke plug with a 14mm socket.

<!-- Figure: Yoke plug removal with 14mm socket, source: ST.pdf page 21 -->

12. Remove the lock nut, yoke plug, rack support spring, rack support yoke and bushing from the gear box.

<!-- Figure: Lock nut, yoke plug, rack support spring, rack support yoke removal showing component labels, source: ST.pdf page 21 -->

13. Remove the valve body housing by loosening the two bolts.

<!-- Figure: Valve body housing bolt removal, source: ST.pdf page 21 -->

14. Turn the rack stopper clockwise until the end of the circlip comes out of the slot in the rack housing.

<!-- Figure: Rack stopper rotation, source: ST.pdf page 21 -->

15. When the end of the circlip comes out of the notched hole of the housing rack cylinder, turn the rack stopper counterclockwise and remove the circlip.

> **CAUTION**
> Be careful not to damage the rack.

<!-- Figure: Circlip removal from rack housing showing circlip and slot positions, source: ST.pdf page 21 -->

16. Remove the rack stopper, rack bushing and rack from the rack housing.

17. Remove the O-ring from the rack bushing.

<!-- ST-22 -->

18. Remove the oil seal from the rack bushing.

<!-- Figure: Oil seal removal from rack bushing showing rack bushing and oil seal, source: ST.pdf page 22 -->

19. Remove the valve body from the valve body housing with a soft hammer.

20. Using the special tool, remove the oil seal and ball bearing from the valve body housing.

21. Remove the oil seal and O-ring from the rack housing.

> **CAUTION**
> Be careful not to damage the pinion valve cylinder bore of the rack housing.

22. Using the special tool, remove the oil seal from the rack housing.

> **CAUTION**
> Be careful not to damage the rack cylinder bore of the rack housing.

### Inspection <!-- SPKC2600 -->

**1. Rack**
   1) Check for rack tooth face damage or wear
   2) Check for oil seal contact surface damage
   3) Check for rack bending or twisting
   4) Check for oil seal ring damage or wear
   5) Check for oil seal damage or wear

<!-- Figure: Rack inspection showing vent hole location, source: ST.pdf page 22 -->

**2. Pinion valve**
   1) Check for pinion gear tooth face damage or wear
   2) Check for oil seal contact surface damage
   3) Check for seal ring damage or wear
   4) Check for oil seal damage or wear

<!-- ST-23 -->

**3. Bearing**
   1) Check for seizure or abnormal noise during bearing rotation
   2) Check for excessive play
   3) Check for missing needle bearing rollers

**4. Others**
   1) Check for damage of the rack housing cylinder bore
   2) Check for boot damage, cracking or aging

### Reassembly <!-- SPKC2700 -->

1. Apply the specified fluid to the entire surface of the rack oil seal.

**Recommended fluid : PSF-3**

2. Install the oil seal to the specified position in the rack housing.

<!-- Figure: Special tools 09573-21200, 09573-21000, 09555-21000, and 09573-21100 for oil seal installation in rack housing, source: ST.pdf page 23 -->

3. Apply the specified fluid to the entire surface of the rack bushing oil seal.

**Recommended fluid : PSF-3**

4. Install the oil seal in the rack bushing.

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09434-14200 installing oil seal in rack bushing with O-ring, source: ST.pdf page 23 -->

5. Apply the specified fluid to the entire surface of the O-ring and install it in the rack bushing.

<!-- ST-24 -->

6. Apply the specified grease to the rack teeth.

**Recommended grease:**
Multipurpose grease SAE J310a, NLGI #2 EP

> **NOTE**
> Do not plug the vent hole in the rack with grease.

<!-- Figure: Rack with vent hole identified, source: ST.pdf page 24 -->

7. Insert the rack into the rack housing and install the rack bushing and rack stopper.

<!-- Figure: Rack bushing and rack stopper installation into rack housing, source: ST.pdf page 24 -->

8. Push in the rack stopper until the circlip groove of the rack stopper is aligned with the notched hole of the rack housing. Install the circlip while turning the rack stopper.

> **CAUTION**
> The circlip end should not be visible through the notched hole of the rack housing.

<!-- Figure: Circlip installation at rack stopper showing circlip, slot, and circlip position, source: ST.pdf page 24 -->

9. Using a special tool, install the oil seal and the ball bearing in the valve body housing.

<!-- Figure: Oil seal and ball bearing installation in valve body housing, source: ST.pdf page 24 -->

10. After applying the specified fluid and grease to the pinion valve assembly, install it in the rack housing assembly.

<!-- Figure: Pinion valve assembly installation, source: ST.pdf page 24 -->

11. After applying the specified fluid to the oil seal, install it in the rack housing, and fix the valve body assembly and O-ring in the gear box.

<!-- ST-25 -->

12. Install the tab washer and the tie rod and stake the tab washer and at two points over the tie rod.

<!-- Figure: Tab washer staking at two points over tie rod, source: ST.pdf page 25 -->

13. Install the bushing, rack support yoke, rack support spring and yoke plug in the order shown in the illustration. Apply semi-drying sealant to the threaded section of the yoke plug before installation.

<!-- Figure: Bushing, rack support yoke, rack support spring, and yoke plug installation order, source: ST.pdf page 25 -->

14. With the rack placed in the center position, attach the yoke plug to the rack housing. Tighten the yoke plug to 7.2 Nm (120 kg.cm, 8.8 lb.ft), with a 14mm socket. Loosen the yoke plug approximately from 30° to 60° and tighten the yoke nut to the specified torque.

**Tightening torque**

50-70 Nm (500-700 kg.cm, 37-52 lb.ft)

<!-- Figure: Special tool 09565-21102 tightening yoke plug, source: ST.pdf page 25 -->

15. Tighten the feed tube to the specified torque and install the mounting rubber cover using adhesive.

16. Apply the specified grease to the bellows mounting position (fitting groove) of the tie rod.

**Recommended grease : Silicone grease**

17. Install the new attaching band to the bellows.

> **NOTE**
> When the bellows are installed, a new band must be used.

18. Install the bellows in position, taking care not to twist.

19. Fit the dust cover inner side and fix with the specified grease, and fix the dust cover in position with the clip ring attached in the groove of the tie rod end.

**Recommended grease**

a : POLY LUB GLY 801K or equivalent
b : BROWN SUNLIGHT MR2 or equivalent

Dust cover inner side and tip : THREE BONDS

<!-- Figure: Dust cover installation showing Grease A and Grease B application points with sealant, source: ST.pdf page 25 -->

<!-- ST-26 -->

20. Install the tie rods so that the length of the left and right tie rods equals the standard value.

**Standard value**

Tie rod free length : 204.5mm

<!-- Figure: Tie rod free length measurement showing 204.5 mm, source: ST.pdf page 26 -->

21. Check for total pinion preload.

### Installation <!-- EPKC0840 -->

1. Push in the power steering gear box assembly in the right side of the vehicle.

<!-- Figure: Gear box installation from right side, source: ST.pdf page 26 -->

2. Install the dust cover mounting plate.

3. Connect the dust cover to its mounting plate with a new strap.

4. Connect the steering gear box assembly to the steering column and shaft assembly.

<!-- Figure: Steering column shaft connection to gear box, source: ST.pdf page 26 -->

5. Installation is the reverse of removal.

6. After installation, bleed the system.

### Removal and Installation <!-- ETKC0900 -->

While installing the tube and hose assembly, be sure to align white marks on each fitting.

<!-- Figure: White line alignment marks on tube fittings, source: ST.pdf page 28 -->

**Pressure Hose and Tube**

1. After draining the power steering fluid, remove the hose mounting clamp(s).

2. Lift up the vehicle and remove the mounting clamp which is holding both the pressure tube and the return tube.

<!-- Figure: Mounting clamp removal holding pressure and return tubes, source: ST.pdf page 28 -->

3. Detach the pressure tube fitting in the gear box.

4. Lower the vehicle and remove the connector.

<!-- Figure: Connector removal at gear box, source: ST.pdf page 28 -->

5. Remove the pressure hose and tube assembly.

6. Installation is the reverse of removal.

> **NOTE**
> Install the pressure hose and tube so that they are not twisted and they do not come in contact with any other parts.

7. After installation, bleed the system.

**Return Tube and Hose**

1. Detach the hose from the power steering fluid reservoir.

<!-- Figure: Return hose disconnection from reservoir, source: ST.pdf page 28 -->

2. Remove the return tube and hose mounting clamps.

3. Raise the vehicle and remove the mounting clamp which is holding both the pressure tube and the return tube.

<!-- Figure: Mounting clamp removal, source: ST.pdf page 28 -->

<!-- ST-29 -->

4. Remove the pressure tube and the return tube fitting in the gear box.

5. Lower the vehicle and disconnect the clamp holding the return tube and hoses.

6. Remove the return tube and hoses.

7. Installation is the reverse of removal.

> **NOTE**
> 1. Install the return tube and hose so that they are not twisted and they do not come in contact with any other parts.
> 2. After installation, air bleed the system.

### Inspection <!-- STKC2600 -->

1. Twisting the hose by hand, check for cracks.

2. Check that the hose does not contact with other components.

### How to Fill with Power Steering Fluid

1. Fill the power steering reservoir with the power steering fluid to the "Max." position.

2. Lift the front wheels with a jack and turn the steering wheel fully to the left and right, 3-5 times for 15-20 sec., while driving the pulley only by operating the start motor.

3. Start the engine, let it idle and turn the steering wheel fully left and right several times until bubbles disappear from the power steering reservoir.

4. If the oil color does not become milk-white and the oil level is constant at the "MAX" position, fluid level is O.K.

> **CAUTION**
> If the oil level changes when turning the steering wheel, and the oil overflows when stopping the engine, it shows that air bleeding wasn't performed perfectly. As it can cause noise and early damage, you must repeat the above procedures.

## Power Steering Oil Pump

### Components <!-- STKB2700 -->

<!-- Figure: Power steering oil pump exploded view showing: pulley, pulley nut, snap ring, suction pipe, dust spacer, connector assembly, mounting bolt, side plate spring, O-ring (inner), driveshaft, oil seal, vane, lock pin, rotor, front housing, O-ring (outer), oil pump cover assembly, oil pump side plate, cam ring, gasket, mounting bolt, source: ST.pdf page 30 -->

### Removal <!-- SPKC2800 -->

1. Remove the pressure hose from the oil pump. Disconnect the suction hose from the suction connector and drain the fluid into a container.

<!-- Figure: Pressure hose and suction hose disconnection from oil pump, source: ST.pdf page 31 -->

2. Loosen the power steering "V" belt tension adjusting bolt.

<!-- Figure: V-belt tension adjusting bolt location, source: ST.pdf page 31 -->

3. Remove the "V" belt from the power steering oil pump pulley.

<!-- Figure: V-belt removal from pump pulley, source: ST.pdf page 31 -->

4. After remove the power steering oil pump mounting bolts and the tension adjusting bolt, remove the power steering oil pump assembly.

<!-- Figure: Oil pump mounting bolt removal, source: ST.pdf page 31 -->

5. Remove the power steering oil pump mounting bracket.

<!-- Figure: Mounting bracket removal, source: ST.pdf page 31 -->

<!-- ST-32 -->
### Disassembly <!-- SPKC2900 -->

1. After removing the 2 bolts, remove the suction pipe and the O-ring from the oil pump body.

<!-- Figure: Suction pipe and O-ring removal, source: ST.pdf page 32 -->

2. Loosen the four bolts and remove the oil pump cover assembly.

3. Remove the cam ring.

4. Remove the rotor and vanes.

5. Remove the oil pump side plate.

<!-- Figure: Oil pump disassembly showing rotor, vanes, cam ring, and side plate, source: ST.pdf page 32 -->

6. Remove the inner and outer O-ring.

7. Remove side plate spring.

> **NOTE**
> When assembling, use a new gasket and O-ring.

<!-- Figure: O-ring and side plate spring removal, source: ST.pdf page 32 -->

8. Mount the pulley in a vise and remove the pulley nut and the spring washer.

<!-- Figure: Pulley held in vise for nut removal, source: ST.pdf page 32 -->

9. Pull the pulley out.

10. After removing the snap ring of the shaft with snap ring pliers, remove the dust spacer.

<!-- Figure: Snap ring and dust spacer removal, source: ST.pdf page 32 -->

11. Tap the rotor side of the shaft slightly with a plastic hammer to remove the shaft.

<!-- Figure: Shaft removal with plastic hammer, source: ST.pdf page 32 -->

<!-- ST-33 -->

12. Remove the oil seal from the oil pump body.

<!-- Figure: Oil seal removal from pump body, source: ST.pdf page 33 -->

13. Remove the connector from the oil pump body, and take out the flow control valve and the flow control spring.

14. Remove the O-ring from the connector.

> **CAUTION**
> Do not disassemble the flow control valve.

<!-- Figure: Flow control valve and spring removal from connector, source: ST.pdf page 33 -->

### Inspection <!-- SPKC3000 -->

1. Check the free length of the flow control spring.

Free length of the flow control spring : 36.5mm

<!-- Figure: Flow control spring free length measurement, source: ST.pdf page 33 -->

2. Check that the flow control valve is not bent.

3. Check the shaft for wear and damage.

4. Check the V-belt for wear and deterioration.

5. Check the grooves of the rotor and vanes for stratified abrasion.

6. Check the contact surface of the cam ring and vanes for stratified abrasion.

7. Check vanes for damage.

8. Check that there is no striped wear in the side plate or contacting part between the shaft and the pump cover surface.

<!-- ST-34 -->
### Reassembly <!-- SPKC3600 -->

1. Install the flow control spring, the flow control valve and the connector into the pump body.

<!-- Figure: Flow control valve and spring installation, source: ST.pdf page 34 -->

2. Install the oil seal in the pump body by using the special tool.

<!-- Figure: Oil seal installation with special tool, source: ST.pdf page 34 -->

3. After inserting the shaft assembly into the pump body, install the dust spacer and snap ring.

<!-- Figure: Shaft assembly, dust spacer, and snap ring installation, source: ST.pdf page 34 -->

4. Install the pump pulley.

5. Install the spring and inner O-ring.

6. Insert the outer O-ring in the oil pump side plate and then install it in the pump body.

<!-- Figure: Front side plate with O-ring and spring installation, source: ST.pdf page 34 -->

<!-- ST-35 -->

7. After inserting the lock pin into the groove of the front housing, install the cam ring attaching to the direction.

<!-- Figure: Lock pin, front housing side, and oil pump cover side installation showing proper direction, source: ST.pdf page 35 -->

8. Install the rotor.

9. Install vanes so that the rounded edges face outward.

<!-- Figure: Vane installation showing oil pump cover side, flat portion, and round portion orientation, source: ST.pdf page 35 -->

10. Install the gasket and oil pump cover assembly.

11. Install the suction pipe and O-ring.

### Installation <!-- STKC2400 -->

1. After installing the oil pump to the oil pump bracket, install the "V" belt and tighten the bolt adjusting tension to the specified torque.

**Oil pump adjusting bolt**

1.6DOHC : 2.5-3.0 Nm (250-300 kg.cm, 18.4-22.0 lb.ft)
2.0DOHC : 3.5-5.0 Nm (350-500 kg.cm, 25.8-37 lb.ft)

<!-- Figure: Oil pump and V-belt installation on engine, source: ST.pdf page 35 -->

2. Install the suction hose.

> **CAUTION**
> Install the pressure hose to the oil pump, facing the painted part on the hose toward the oil pump.

3. Install the pressure hose to the oil pump.

> **NOTE**
> Install the pressure hose being careful so that it does not twist and come in contact with other components.

<!-- Figure: Pressure hose installation at oil pump, source: ST.pdf page 35 -->

4. Add power steering fluid (PSF-3).

5. Air bleed the system.

6. Check the oil pump pressure.
