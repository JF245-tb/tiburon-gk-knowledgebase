### Disassembly
<!-- EAOB0611 -->

1. Take off a bearing using special tool (09527-4A000).

<!-- Figure: Bearing removal with special tool, source: TR.pdf page 96 -->

2. Remove a snap ring.

<!-- Figure: Snap ring removal, source: TR.pdf page 96 -->

3. Remove the 6th gear, bush, needle bearing and spacer using special tool (09457-34000).

<!-- Figure: 6th gear removal with special tool 09457-34000, source: TR.pdf page 96 -->

4. Pull out the 5th gear and the coupling assembly at the same time using special tool (09457-34000).

<!-- Figure: 5th gear and coupling assembly removal, source: TR.pdf page 96 -->

5. Remove the needle bearing and spacer.

<!-- Figure: Needle bearing and spacer removal, source: TR.pdf page 96 -->

6. Pull out the 3rd/4th gear and the coupling assembly at the same time using special tool (09432-2C300).

<!-- Figure: 3rd/4th gear and coupling assembly removal with tool 09432-2C300, source: TR.pdf page 96 -->

7. Remove the needle bearing and spacer.

8. Pull out the input shaft front bearing.

<!-- Figure: Input shaft front bearing removal, source: TR.pdf page 96 -->

### Reassembly
<!-- EAOB0641 -->

1. Assemble the input shaft front bearing using special tool (09432-33300).

<!-- Figure: Input shaft front bearing assembly with tool 09432-33300, source: TR.pdf page 96 -->

2. Assemble the needle bearing.

3. Put in the input 3rd gear and the 3rd synchronizer.

> Keep wrong position of spring's slit at the face and back.

4. Put sleeve coupling with projecting side upper.

<!-- Figure: Sleeve coupling orientation, source: TR.pdf page 97 -->

5. Put down 3 grooves hub, and press on the shaft.

> **NOTE**
> At this time, set 3rd synchronizer ring projecting and hub notch.

6. Press on a input 4th bush using special tool (09432-3A100).

> **NOTE**
> Pay attention to incline the bush easily.

<!-- Figure: Input 4th bush press-on with tool 09432-3A100, source: TR.pdf page 97 -->

7. Select correct thrust washer ring as "L" dimension becomes below basic dimension and put it on input shaft.

**L basic dimension (mm): 154.7 - 154.8**

#### Thrust Washer Ring

| Thickness (mm) |
|:---:|
| 3.84 |
| 3.90 |
| 3.96 |
| 4.02 |
| 4.08 |
| 4.14 |

<!-- Figure: Thrust washer ring selection and installation, source: TR.pdf page 97 -->

8. Assemble the needle bearing and spacer.

9. Assemble 4th synchronizer ring.

10. Put on input 4th gear.

11. Assemble the needle bearing and spacer.

<!-- Figure: Needle bearing assembly, source: TR.pdf page 98 -->

12. Press on 5th bush using special tool (09432-3A100).

> **NOTE**
> Pay attention to incline the bush easily.

13. Assemble a needle bearing.

14. Put in the 5th input gear and 5th synchronizer.

<!-- Figure: 5th input gear and synchronizer assembly, source: TR.pdf page 98 -->

15. Put the sleeve coupling with discrimination groove side upper.

<!-- Figure: Sleeve coupling orientation with discrimination groove, source: TR.pdf page 98 -->

> Discrimination groove shown between 6th input gear side and 5th input gear side on the 5th-6th coupling sleeve.

16. Press on coupling assembly.

17. Put in the 6th synchronizer ring.

<!-- Figure: 6th synchronizer ring installation, source: TR.pdf page 98 -->

18. Spread grease to the bush, and assemble a needle bearing and a spacer.

19. Put 6th gear and press on the bush using special tool (09432-3A100).

<!-- Figure: 6th gear press-on with tool 09432-3A100, source: TR.pdf page 98 -->

20. Select the correct snap ring of 6th input to minimize clearance of groove in input shaft and then assemble it.

**L (mm): 0 - 0.1**

### Reassembly
<!-- EAOB0911 -->

1. Assemble the reverse gear using special tool (09432-2C100).

<!-- Figure: Reverse gear assembly with press and tool 09432-2C100, source: TR.pdf page 102 -->

2. Assemble a 1st bush using special tool (09432-2C100).

<!-- Figure: 1st bush assembly with press and tool 09432-2C100, source: TR.pdf page 102 -->

3. Assemble a needle bearing.

<!-- Figure: Needle bearing assembly, source: TR.pdf page 102 -->

4. Assemble a 1st gear using special tool (09432-2C100).

<!-- Figure: 1st gear assembly with press and tool 09432-2C100, source: TR.pdf page 102 -->

5. Assemble a synchronizer. The synchronizer ring for 1st gear is double cone type.

> **NOTE**
> Pay attention the direction of protrusion of a synchronizer ring.

<!-- Figure: Synchronizer assembly showing double cone type for 1st gear, source: TR.pdf page 102 -->

6. Put 1st/2nd sleeve coupling assembly with projecting side lower.

<!-- Figure: 1st/2nd coupling sleeve orientation, source: TR.pdf page 102 -->

> 2nd gear side has 3 grooves near the spline for 1st gear side, and 4 grooves for 2nd side.

7. There are 3 grooves near the spline for 1st gear side, and 4 grooves for 2nd side.

8. Put sleeve coupling with projecting side lower, and also pay attention to set a hub notch with coupling notch.

9. Press on coupling assembly using special tool (09432-3A100).

> **NOTE**
> Keep under side 3 grooves of a hub.

10. Pay attention the protrusion of synchronizer ring is engaged with hub notch.

<!-- Figure: Coupling assembly press-on with tool 09432-3A100, source: TR.pdf page 103 -->

11. Assemble a 2nd bush using special tool (09432-3A100).

> **NOTE**
> Pay attention to the bush's inclining.

<!-- Figure: 2nd bush assembly with press and tool 09432-3A100, source: TR.pdf page 103 -->

12. Assemble a synchronizer ring and needle bearing on output shaft.

13. Assemble a 2nd gear on the output shaft.

<!-- Figure: 2nd gear assembly on output shaft, source: TR.pdf page 103 -->

14. Assemble 3rd gear using special tool (09432-3A100).

> **NOTE**
> Pay attention as a groove direction keeps upper.

<!-- Figure: 3rd gear assembly with tool 09432-3A100, source: TR.pdf page 103 -->

15. Select the correct adjust shim of 4th gear as "L" dimension becomes below basic dimension and assemble it.

### Disassembly
<!-- EAOB0600 -->

1. Remove a stopper bolt and a shift check.

<!-- Figure: Stopper bolt and shift check removal, source: TR.pdf page 107 -->

2. Loosen 2 bolts, and remove a spring and a ball and a pin with a magnet tool.

<!-- Figure: Spring, ball and pin removal with magnet tool, source: TR.pdf page 107 -->

3. Take off 4 bolts with shifting any position to loosen bolts.

<!-- Figure: 4 bolts removal from transaxle case, source: TR.pdf page 107 -->

4. Take off the reverse switch.

<!-- Figure: Reverse switch removal, source: TR.pdf page 107 -->

5. Remove a control assembly.

> **NOTE**
> At this time, it comes to pull out easily if you pull out a control assembly while moving to select direction.

<!-- Figure: Control assembly removal, source: TR.pdf page 107 -->

6. Remove a bore plug by a minus driver etc.

<!-- Figure: Bore plug removal, source: TR.pdf page 107 -->

7. Take off the snap ring of the output shaft from the hole after removing a bore plug.

> **NOTE**
> The output shaft drops with the sound named by expanding the snap ring of the output shaft.

<!-- Figure: Output shaft snap ring removal through bore plug hole, source: TR.pdf page 107 -->
