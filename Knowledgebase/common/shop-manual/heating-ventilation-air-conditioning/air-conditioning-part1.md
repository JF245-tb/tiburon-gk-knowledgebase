# Air Conditioning -- Part 1

## Manual A/C Compressor Controls

### Clutch Field Coil

#### Disassembly

1. Remove the clutch hub and pulley.
2. Install a shaft protection tool at the compressor opening.
3. Install the pulley at the compressor. Place the puller screw end at the shaft protectors center location and the puller projection around the rear side field coil.
4. Turn the puller screw using a wrench and remove the coil.

> **CAUTION**
> Do not use air tools.

<!-- Figure: Clutch field coil removal with puller tool, source: HA.pdf page 21 -->

4. Place shim at specified side at the hub spline opening inside and slide the hub onto the compressor shaft end.
5. Install a new hub supporting bolt on the compressor shaft end. Tighten the bolt to the specified torque.

<!-- Figure: Hub bolt installation on compressor shaft end, source: HA.pdf page 21 -->

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Tightening torque | 110-140 kg-cm |

> **CAUTION**
> Do not use air tools.

### Thermal Fuse

1. The thermal fuse is located on the compressor clutch field coil.
2. Outline:
   The thermal fuse will detect clutch slip heat (184 degrees C off) generated by faulty compressor operation, then interrupt the coil's power supply to stop the clutch operation. Therefore, clutch bearing and pulley bearing damage will not continue in order to protect the belt and engine. Once the thermal fuse interrupts, the compressor power supply will be interrupted and the compressor will not operate. Check the compressor with a tester (3.0-3.5 ohm) to find the thermal fuse condition, and replace the clutch coil if required.

---

### Checking Clutch Air Gap

1. Check clutch air gap between the clutch hub and the pulley contact surface using a filler gauge.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Clutch air gap | 0.35-0.08 mm |

2. Check gaps around the pulley at 3 points.
3. If the clutch air gap is outside the normal range, correct the air gap into the normal range using a shim of the proper size.

<!-- Figure: Checking clutch air gap with filler gauge, source: HA.pdf page 22 -->

**Condenser:**

1. Check condenser pin for clogging and damage. If clogged, clean it with water, and blow it with compressed air. If bent, gently stretch it using a screwdriver or pliers.

1. Check the connector for connection condition.
2. Check the condenser fan motor using battery voltage.

### Receiver/Drier

1. Check the fusible plug and the fittings for leakage, using a leak detector.
2. Run the engine at fast idle with the air conditioning "ON", and check both the inlet and outlet temperatures. If the difference in temperatures between the inlet and outlet is large, replace the receiver-drier.

<!-- Figure: Condenser and receiver drier assembly location, source: HA.pdf page 25 -->

> **NOTE**
> 1. *Plug the all open fittings immediately to keep moisture out of the system.*
> 2. *Do not remove plugs until ready for connection.*
> 3. *If the receiver-drier is replaced with a new unit, add 40cc of compressor oil to the compressor.*
> 4. *Evacuate, charge and test the refrigeration system.*

### Triple Pressure Switch

The triple switch is a combination of a middle switch as well as a low-side/front the pressure and high pressure switches. The low pressure switch will turn off to stop compressor operation if refrigerant pressure is low. The high pressure switch will turn off to stop compressor operation if refrigerant pressure is too high. The middle switch will turn on at a medium level pressure to determine the A/C system is overheating. It will cool the A/C system by operating the radiator fan and the condenser fan at high speed.

#### Operating Characteristic

| Pressure | ON (kg/cm2) | OFF (kg/cm2) |
|---|---|---|
| High | 32.0 +/- 2.0 | 26.0 +/- 2.0 |
| Low | 2.3 +/- 0.25 | 2.0 +/- 0.2 |
| Middle | 19.0 +/- 0.8 | 14 +/- 1.2 |

### Control Panel

#### Air Conditioner Switch (Manual)

<!-- Figure: Manual A/C control panel showing mode knob (left), A/C button (center-left), temperature knob (center-right), and blower speed knob (right), source: HA.pdf page 27 -->

#### Switch Configuration (Manual)

<!-- Figure: Manual HVAC control panel switch layout detail, source: HA.pdf page 28 -->

1. **Blower Switch** -- The blower switch controls the blowing level of the air conditioning system by controlling blower motor speed. The switch has an electrical circuit containing a resistor that will regulate blower motor input voltage to control the motor speed.

2. **Mode Switch** -- The mode switch controls air conditioning system discharge location. The switch contains an electrical circuit to control an actuator that is connected to the mode door for discharge control.

3. **Temperature Switch** -- The temperature switch controls the temperature door position that will be used to regulate the air conditioning system's discharge air temperature. The switch includes a rack & pinion and a cable.

4. **Intake Switch** -- The intake switch controls the intake door used to regulate the intake air flow of the air conditioning system. The switch contains an electrical circuit used to control the actuator that is connected to the intake door.

5. **Air Conditioning Switch** -- The A/C switch controls the on/off position of the air conditioning system compressor. The switch contains an electrical circuit that will switch on/off the power supply to the relay that is connected to the compressor.

6. **Rear Defogger Switch** -- The rear defogger switch is used to defog the rear glass. Switching the switch on, ETACS will output a signal to operate the rear glass heat wire.

## Full Auto A/C Compressor

### Diagnosis System

#### Operation Method (Self-Diagnosis)

The F.A.T.C. module self test feature will detect electrical malfunctions and provide error codes for system components with suspected failures.

**Procedure:**

1. IGN S/W: OFF then ON
2. Press the AMB S/W more than 4 times within 2 seconds while pressing the AUTO S/W.
3. After LCD display blinks 3 times per 0.5 second, start self diagnosis.
4. Self diagnosis modes:
   - **Continuous operation:** Press AUTO S/W
   - **Step operation:** Press OFF S/W
5. A/C system control

### Ambient Temperature Sensor

#### Ambient Air Temperature Sensor

1. The air temperature sensor, located at the front of the engine radiator, detects ambient air temperature. It is a negative type thermistor; resistance will increase with lower temperatures, and decrease with higher temperatures.

2. The sensor output will be used for discharge temperature sensor, sensor fail-safe, temperature regulation door control, blower motor level control, mix mode control and in-car humidity control.

<!-- Figure: Ambient air temperature sensor and mounting location at front of radiator, source: HA.pdf page 35 -->

---

### A.Q.S (Air Quality Sensor)

#### A.Q.S. Sensor

1. The A.Q.S. sensor, located at the center support in front of the engine radiator, detects hazardous elements in ambient air and provides output signals to the control module.

2. It will detect sulfurous acid gas, carbon dioxide, carbon monoxide, hydrocarbon and allergen.

#### Sensor Output

| Condition | Resistance |
|---|---|
| Normal condition | 5V |
| Hazardous gas detection | 0V |
